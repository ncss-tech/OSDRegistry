TAXONOMIC CLASS: Siliceous, hyperthermic Fluventic Psammowassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Eastpoint shelly fine sand on a submerged wave-cut platform under 0.7 meters of brackish water. (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 Cseg1--0 to 26 centimeters; 60 percent gray (2.5Y 5/1), 25 percent very dark grayish brown (2.5Y 3/2), and 15 percent black (10YR 2/1) shelly fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; slightly fluid; 20 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (1.8 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.2), slightly alkaline (pH 7.5) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg3--74 to 134 centimeters; 60 percent very dark grayish brown (2.5Y 3/2), 30 percent dark gray (10YR 4/1), and 10 percent light brownish gray (10YR 6/2) fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 5 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (1.5 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), extremely acid (pH 4.1) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg4--134 to 200 centimeters; 75 percent very dark gray (10YR 3/1) and 25 percent grayish brown (10YR 5/2) loamy fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 2 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.0 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.0), extremely acid (pH 3.6) oxidized pH

COMPETING SERIES:
These are the Broadaxe, Lutz, McKissack, Mullet, Pelican Reef, Sandbank, and Thalinectes series.
Broadaxe soils--Formed on submerged wave-cut platforms and contain buried spodic horizons.
Lutz soils--Formed on flood-tidal deltas from sandy lagoonal deposits and have oxidized pHs greater than 7.8.
McKissack soils--Formed on brackish estuarine tidal streams from eroded spodic soil materials, commonly have hues of 7.5YR and chromas greater than 3, and organic stains between sand grains. 
Mullet soils--Formed on washover-fan flats, commonly have hues of 5GY or greener, and oxidized pHs greater than 6.1.
Pelican Reef soils--Formed on relict flood-tidal deltas from sandy lagoonal deposits and do not have sandy loam lamellae.
Sandbank soils--Formed in nearby estuarine tidal stream deltas in estuarine deposits and have very fine mica flakes throughout
Thalinectes soils--Formed in sandy lagoonal and marine deposits on washover-fans and longshore bars and have higher soil organic carbon densities in the surface horizon.

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Apalachicola, Cash, Dregs, Dry Bar, and Norman soils.
Apalachicola soils--Are on bay and lagoon bottoms and formed in fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Cash soils--Are on mainland coves and formed in fine-loamy estuarine deposits.
Dregs soils--Are on dredge-deposit shoals and formed in sandy, human transported materials
Dry Bar soils--Are on shoals and reefs and formed in sandy-skeletal lagoonal deposits. 
Norman soils--Are on shoals and reefs and formed in loamy-skeletal lagoonal deposits.

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--High to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish or salt water.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--The area is used for recreational fishing and boating. Some areas support commercial shellfish production within embayments. Some areas support subaquatic vegetation where wave-energy dissipates before reaching the shoreline. Eastpoint soils can be intertidal depending on proximity to oceanic regress. 
Typical vegetation--Vegetation presence is largely determined by wave energy. In areas where turbidity is low, tape grass (Vallisneria spiralis), shoal grass (Halodule wrightii) or widgeon grass (Ruppia maritima) may establish.

REMARKS:
Typical Pedon Taxonomic Features:
Reduced matrix--The zone from 0 to 200 centimeters has a soil color matrix chroma of 2 or less
Fluidity--Slightly fluid from 0 to 26 centimeters
Hyposulfidic materials--The zone from 26 to 134 centimeters has an initial pH that decreases by 1 or more units but remains at a value of 4.0 or more after 16 weeks of moist aerobic incubation
Hypersulfidic materials--The zone from 134 to 200 centimeters has an initial pH that decreased by 1 or more units and is less than 4.0 after 16 weeks of moist aerobic incubation
Peraquic feature--Zero or positive soil water potential at the soil surface (permanently submerged)
