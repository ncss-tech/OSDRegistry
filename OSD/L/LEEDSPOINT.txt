MLRA(s)--153D
Depth Class--Very deep
Drainage Class--Subaqueous (permanently submerged)
Water Depth Range--0 to 2 meters
Parent Material--Coarse-loamy lagoonal deposits over sandy relict flood-tidal delta lagoonal deposits
Slope--0 to 1 percent
Mean Annual Air Temperature--13 degrees C (55 degrees F)
Mean Annual Precipitation--1118 millimeters (44 inches)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-loamy, mixed, subactive, nonacid, mesic Fluventic Sulfiwassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Leedspoint loam on a permanently submerged relict flood-tidal delta under 1.7 meters of saline water(colors are for moist soil unless otherwise noted).

 Aseg--0 to 17 centimeters; 60 percent black (N 2.5/) and 40 percent very dark greenish gray (5GY 3/1) loam; massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; slightly fluid; common medium roots throughout and common fine roots throughout; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; strong effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; electrical conductivity 1.9 dS/m; strongly alkaline (pH 8.6), extremely acid (pH 4.3) oxidized pH; clear boundary. (Thickness of Aseg horizon is 15 to 45 centimeters)

 Cseg1--17 to 69 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (5GY 3/1) loam; massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; slightly fluid; 2 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderate effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; electrical conductivity 2.7 dS/m; moderately alkaline (pH 8.0), extremely acid (pH 3.6) oxidized pH; gradual boundary.

 Cseg2--69 to 91 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (5GY 3/1) fine sandy loam; massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; slightly fluid; moderate sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; electrical conductivity 3.6 dS/m; slightly alkaline (pH 7.7), extremely acid (pH 3.8) oxidized pH; clear boundary. 

 Cseg3--91 to 128 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (5GY 3/1) stratified fine sand to silt loam; massive; moderately sticky, slightly plastic; moderately fluid; moderate sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; electrical conductivity 2.9 dS/m; moderately alkaline (pH 8.0), extremely acid (pH 3.5) oxidized pH; abrupt boundary.

 Cseg4--128 to 151 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (10Y 3/1) fine sandy loam; massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; moderately fluid; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; electrical conductivity 3.4 dS/m; slightly alkaline (pH 7.8), very strongly acid (pH 4.7) oxidized pH; clear boundary. (Total thickness of Cseg horizons is 40 to 213 centimeters).

 2Cseg5--151 to 197 centimeters; very dark gray (N 3/) sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; electrical conductivity 3.4 dS/m; moderately alkaline (pH 8.0), moderately acid (pH 5.8) oxidized pH.

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Absegami, Cottman, and Indian River soils.
Absegami soils--formed in coarse-loamy lagoonal deposits that have a buried surface and formed on shoals adjacent to main navigation channels.
Cottman soils--formed in coarse-loamy lagoonal deposits underlain by fine-silty estuarine deposits and formed on lagoon bottom barrier side landforms. 
Indian River soils--have coarser textures throughout and formed on active flood-tidal delta lagoonal deposits

DRAINAGE AND SATURADED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage Class--Subaqueous 
Saturated Hydraulic Conductivity--moderately high to high in the coarse-loamy deposits, high to very high in the lithologic discontinuity
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated with salt or brackish water.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--Areas of these soils are used for recreational fishing, hunting, and boating. Commercial use includes shell fishing and aquaculture. These soils also serve as aquatic wildlife habitat for tubeworms, clams, juvenile blue crabs, scallops and juvenile finfish.  
Typical vegetation--rooted algae, eelgrass (Zostera marina), and widgeon grass (Ruppia maritima)

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: 
General area--Coastal bays of the Southern New Jersey coastal plain, on relic flood-tidal deltas where inlets have closed or migrated due to longshore transport.

REMARKS: These soils were previously included and mapped as miscellaneous areas of Water, saline. Leeds Point is an unincorporated village in coastal Atlantic County that has many historical ties to the settlement and founding of coastal Atlantic County.  Also, the birthplace of the mythical Jersey Devil.  

Diagnostic horizons and features in this pedon include-
Ochric Epipedon--the zone from 0 to 17 centimeters (Aseg horizon)
Hypersulfidic materials--The zone from 17 to 128 centimeters (Cseg1, Cseg2, Cseg3 horizons) that has an initial pH that decreases to a value less than 4.0 after 16 weeks of moist aerobic incubation
Hyposulfidic materials-the zones from 0 to 17 and 128 to 197 centimeters (Aseg, Cseg4, 2Cseg5 horizons) that has an initial pH that decreases by 1 or more units but remains at a value of 4.0 or more after 16 weeks of moist aerobic incubation
Lithologic discontinuity--coarse-loamy lagoonal deposits overlying sandy flood-tidal delta lagoonal deposits from 151 to 197 centimeters (2Cseg5 horizon).
Fluidity--The zone from 69 to 151 centimeters has a fluidity class of slightly fluid to moderately fluid
Peraquic feature--Zero or positive soil water potential at the soil surface (permanently submerged).
