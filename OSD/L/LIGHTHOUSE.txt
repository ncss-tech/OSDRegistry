LIGHTHOUSE SERIES

MLRA(s)--152A
Depth class--Very deep
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Water depth range--0 to 3 meters (0 to 10 feet)
Parent material--Loamy-skeletal lagoonal deposits
Geomorphic location--Shoals and reefs
Slope range--0 to 1 percent
Mean annual air temperature--21 degrees C (70 degrees F)
Mean annual water temperature--24 degrees C (75 degrees F)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, mixed, active, nonacid, hyperthermic Grossic Hydrowassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Norman very shelly coarse sandy loam on a shoal under 2.0 meters of brackish water (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 Cseg1--0 to 35 centimeters; very dark grayish brown (2.5Y 3/2) very shelly coarse sandy loam; massive; nonsticky, nonplastic; very fluid; 55 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (3.7 dS/m); strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), moderately alkaline (pH 8.2) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg2--35 to 88 centimeters; very dark gray (5Y 3/1) extremely shelly sandy clay loam; massive; nonsticky, nonplastic; moderately fluid; 75 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (4.5 dS/m); strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), moderately alkaline (pH 8.2) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg3--88 to 131 centimeters; greenish black (10Y 2.5/1) very shelly sandy clay loam; massive; slightly sticky, nonplastic; moderately fluid; 55 percent shell fragments; moderate sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (4.0 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.4), moderately alkaline (pH 8.2) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg4--131 to 184 centimeters; very dark gray (5Y 3/1) extremely shelly sandy clay loam; massive; slightly sticky, nonplastic; moderately fluid; 70 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (3.8 dS/m); strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), moderately alkaline (pH 8.3) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg5--184 to 226 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (10Y 3/1) shells; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 95 percent shell fragments; strong sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (4.9 dS/m); strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), moderately alkaline (pH 8.4) oxidized pH

TYPE LOCATION: Franklin County, Florida; East Bay; approximately 2.6kilometers ( miles) west of the terminus of Old Ferry Dock Road, approximately 5.9 kilometers (3.7 miles) east of the intersection of Avenue I and Water Street, and 9.9 kilometers (6.1 miles) north of Saint George Island Airport-14 Runway; USGS topographic quadrangle: Apalachicola, FL.

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Landscape--Bays and lagoons
Landform--Shoals and reefs
Parent material--Loamy-skeletal lagoonal deposits
Slope--0 to 1 percent
Water depth range--0 to 3 meters (0 to 10 feet) below mean sea level
Water salinity range--15 to 30 parts per thousand
Soil temperature--16 to 31 degrees C (61 to 88 degrees F)
Water temperature--18 to 28 degrees C (64 to 82 degrees F)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Apalachicola, Dry Bar, and Eastpoint soils.
Apalachicola soils--Formed on bay and lagoon bottoms from fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Dry Bar soils--Formed on shoals and reefs from sandy-skeletal lagoonal deposits and are nonfluid throughout.
Eastpoint soils--formed on submerged wave-cut platforms from sandy lagoonal deposits and have hyposulfidic materials.

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately high to high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish or salt water

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--Commercial uses include production and harvesting Eastern oysters (Crassostrea virginica). Recreational uses include oyster harvesting and fishing.
Typical vegetation--Vegetation is rarely observed on Lighthouse soils due to the prevalence of active and inactive oyster shell beds occurring on the surface.      

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT:
General area--Brackish and saltwater shoals and reefs along the Gulf.
Land Resource Regions--T - Atlantic and Gulf Coast Lowland Forest and Crop Region

REMARKS:
Typical Pedon Taxonomic Features:
Reduced matrix--The zone from 0 to 226 centimeters has a soil color matrix with a chroma of 2 or less.
Fluidity--The zone from 0 to 184 has a fluidity class of slightly fluid to very fluid.
Peraquic feature--Zero or positive soil water potential at the soil surface (permanently submerged).
Taxonomic Version: Keys to Soil Taxonomy, Thirteenth Edition, 2022
Other: The series is named after Lighthouse Bar, a historic oyster reef where first observed. Lighthouse soils were previously mapped as water in Florida soil surveys.
