LUTZ SERIES

Depth class--Very deep
Drainage class--Subaqueous

Parent material--Sandy lagoonal deposits
Geomorphic location--Flood-tidal delta
Slope range--0 to 1 percent
Mean annual air temperature--21 degrees C (70 degrees F)
Mean annual water temperature--24 degrees C (75 degrees F)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Siliceous, hyperthermic Fluventic Psammowassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Lutz fine sand on a flood-tidal delta under 0.5 meters of salt water. (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 Cseg1--0 to 11 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (5GY 3/1) fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderate effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.6 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.2), moderately alkaline (pH 8.0) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg2--11 to 91 centimeters; dark gray (5Y 4/1) sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; few very fine roots; 2 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.5 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.2), slightly alkaline (pH 7.8) oxidized pH; gradual boundary

 Cseg3--91 to 162 centimeters; dark olive gray (5Y 3/2) sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 5 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.6 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), moderately alkaline (pH 8.2) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cg--162 to 195 centimeters; 70 percent dark olive gray (5Y 3/2) and 30 percent dark gray (2.5Y 4/1) sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 10 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; no effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (3.0 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.2), moderately alkaline (pH 8.4) oxidized

COMPETING SERIES:
These are the Broadaxe, Eastpoint, McKissack, Mullet, Pelican Reef, Sandbank, and Thalinectes series.
Broadaxe soils--Formed on submerged wave-cut platforms and contain buried spodic horizons.
Eastpoint soils--Formed on submerged wave-cut platforms and can have oxidized pHs below 7.4.
McKissack soils--Formed on brackish estuarine tidal streams from eroded spodic soil materials, commonly have hues of 7.5YR and chromas greater than 3, and organic stains between sand grains. 
Mullet soils--Formed on washover-fan flats and have higher organic carbon densities in the surface.
Pelican Reef soils--Formed on relict flood-tidal deltas and have higher organic carbon densities in the surface.
Sandbank soils--Formed on estuarine tidal stream deltas where the major rivers meet the estuary, have higher organic carbon content and allow loamy sand textures
Thalinectes soils--Formed in sandy lagoonal and marine deposits on washover-fans and longshore bars and have higher soil organic carbon densities in the surface. 

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Landscape--Bays and Lagoons along the Gulf Coast
Landform--Flood tidal-deltas
Parent material--Sandy lagoonal deposits 
Slope--0 to 1 percent
Water depth range--0 to 2 meters (0 to 6 feet) below mean sea level
Water salinity range--20 to 35 parts per thousand
Water temperature range--18 to 30 degrees C (64 to 86 degrees F)
Soil temperature range--19 to 27 degrees C (66 to 81 degrees F)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Apalachicola, Dregs, Dry Bar, and Sheepshead soils.
Apalachicola soils--Are on bay and lagoon bottoms and formed in fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Dregs soils--Are on dredge-deposit shoals and formed in sandy, human transported materials
Dry Bar soils--Are on shoals and reefs and formed in sandy-skeletal lagoonal deposits. 
Sheepshead soils--Are in barrier coves and formed in sandy and coarse-loamy lagoonal deposits. 

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--High to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish or salt water.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--These soils are used for recreational fishing and are dredged for commercial passage. Lutz soils can be intertidal depending on depositional patterns. 
Typical vegetation--Vegetation is largely absent on Lutz soils. Where lower wave-energy persists, limited stands of shoal grass (Halodule wrightii) may establish.
