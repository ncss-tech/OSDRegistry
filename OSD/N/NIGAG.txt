NIGAG SERIES

Landscape--mountains, hills
Landform--mountain slopes, hillslopes
Slope--15 to 40 percent
Parent material-colluvium influenced by volcanic ash over till
Mean annual precipitation--about 1200 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 0 degrees C
Depth class--moderately deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--udic
Soil temperature regime--cryic
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Medial over loamy-skeletal, ferrihydritic over isotic Andic Humicryods

TYPICAL PEDON: Nigag silt loam on south-southwest-facing, convex glaciated mountain footslope, on a slope of 15 percent at an elevation of 216 m (The soil was moist throughout when described on July 26, 2012.)

 Oe--0 to 9 cm; moderately decomposed plant material; nonsticky and nonplastic; common fine and medium and many very fine and coarse roots; very strongly acid (pH 4.9); clear wavy boundary

 E/A--9 to 12 cm; ashy silt loam; E part--55 percent pink (5YR 7/3) dry, reddish brown (5YR 5/3) moist, weak medium subangular blocky structure, slightly hard, friable, nonsticky and nonplastic, common very fine to medium roots, 3 percent gravel, very strongly acid (pH 4.8), clear wavy boundary; A part--45 percent brown (7.5YR 4/2) dry, very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2) moist, weak medium subangular blocky structure, slightly hard, friable, nonsticky and nonplastic, common very fine to medium roots, 3 percent gravel, very strongly acid (pH 4.8), clear wavy boundary

 Bhs1--12 to 24 cm; medial silt loam, 60 percent dark gray (5YR 4/1) dry, black (5YR 2.5/1) moist, and 40 percent reddish brown (5YR 5/3) dry, dark reddish brown (5YR 3/3) moist; weak fine subangular blocky structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine to medium roots; 3 percent gravel; very strongly acid (pH 4.8); clear wavy boundary

 Bhs2--24 to 44 cm; medial silt loam, brown (7.5YR 4/3) dry, very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/3) moist; weak medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine and fine roots; 5 percent gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.3); gradual smooth boundary

 2BC--44 to 72 cm; very gravelly sandy loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak fine granular structure; soft, very friable, slightly sticky and nonplastic; common very fine and fine roots; 40 percent gravel; moderately acid (pH 5.8); clear smooth boundary

 2Cd--72 to 150 cm; very gravelly loamy coarse sand, pale brown (10YR 6/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; massive; hard, friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; 50 percent gravel; moderately acid (pH 5.8); clear smooth boundary

TYPE LOCATION: Bethel Census Area, Alaska, about 50 km north-northwest of the village of Togiak; about 565 m south and 32 m west of the northeast corner of section 19, T. 8 S., R. 66 W., Seward Meridian; Goodnews Bay B-5 U.S. Geological Survey quadrangle; latitude 59.4711111 degrees north, longitude 160.5611111 degrees west, datum WGS 84 (coordinates determined with a GPS)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
