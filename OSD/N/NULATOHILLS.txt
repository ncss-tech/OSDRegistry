NULATOHILLS SERIES

Landscape--mountains, hills
Landform--mountain slopes, hillslopes
Slope--16 to 70 percent
Parent material--colluvium derived from volcanic ash over gravelly colluvium   
Mean annual precipitation--about 550 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about -4 degrees C
Depth class--very deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--udic
Soil temperature regime--cryic
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Medial over loamy-skeletal, amorphic over mixed, superactive Andic Humicryepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Nulatohills silt loam on an east-southeast facing linear, linear backslope on a hill, on a slope of 30 percent at an elevation of 454 m (The soil was moist throughout when described on August 4, 2019.)

 Oe--0 to 5 cm; moderately decomposed plant material, dark gray (7.5YR 4/1) dry, black (7.5YR 2.5/1) moist; nonsmeary; common very fine, fine, medium, and coarse roots throughout ; extremely acid (pH 3.6); clear smooth boundary

 A--5 to 30 cm; medial silt loam,   brown (7.5YR 4/2) dry, very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2) moist; weak coarse subangular blocky structure; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; moderately smeary; common very fine, fine, and coarse roots throughout, and few medium roots throughout; few very fine tubular and pores ; 10 percent subangular gravel; extremely acid (pH 4.3); abrupt smooth boundary

 2Bw1--30 to 50 cm; very gravelly loam, pale brown (10YR 6/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; moderate fine subangular blocky structure; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; few very fine roots throughout; common very fine tubular pores; silt coats; 5 percent subangular cobbles by volume, 35 percent subangular gravel; very strongly acid (pH 4.8); clear smooth boundary

 2Bw2--50 to 150 cm; very gravelly loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak coarse granular structure; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; strongly smeary; few very fine tubular pores; 55 percent subangular gravel; very strongly acid (pH 4.8)
