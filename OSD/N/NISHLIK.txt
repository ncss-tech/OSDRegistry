NISHLIK SERIES

Landscape--plains, hills
Landform--rises, hillslopes
Slope--3 to 40 percent
Parent material--loess influenced by volcanic ash over glacial drift
Mean annual precipitation--about 1000 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about -1 degrees C
Depth class--very deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--udic
Soil temperature regime--cryic
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, isotic Typic Humicryods

TYPICAL PEDON: Nishlik silt loam on a west-facing, linear rise of a glaciated plain, on a slope of 5 percent at an elevation of 389 m (The soil was moist throughout when described on July 28, 2008.)

 Oi--0 to 9 cm; slightly decomposed plant material; many very fine and fine and common medium and coarse roots; very strongly acid (pH 4.6); clear smooth boundary

 E--9 to 14 cm; silt loam, gray (10YR 6/1) dry, dark gray (10YR 4/1) moist; weak medium granular structure; slightly hard, friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine to coarse roots; 5 percent gravel; very strongly acid (pH 4.9); clear smooth boundary

 Bhs--14 to 27 cm; medial silt loam, gray (5YR 5/1) dry, very dark gray (5YR 3/1) moist; weak fine granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine to medium roots; 5 percent gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.5); clear wavy boundary

 2Bw--27 to 32 cm; sandy loam, light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) moist; weak fine granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; few very fine and fine roots; 10 percent gravel; moderately acid (pH 5.8); clear wavy boundary

 2C--32 to 180 cm; very gravelly sandy loam, light yellowish brown (2.5Y 6/3) dry, olive brown (2.5Y 4/3) moist; massive; slightly hard, friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; 3 percent cobbles and 35 percent gravel; slightly acid (pH 6.1)

TYPE LOCATION: Dillingham Census Area, Alaska, about 125 km north of the city of Dillingham; latitude 60.1905556 degrees north, longitude 158.7313889 degrees west, datum WGS 84 (coordinates determined with a GPS)
