NIKAAT SERIES

Landscape--valley
Landform--loess covered terraces and plains
Slope--0 to 3 percent
Parent material--cryoturbated organic material and loess
Mean annual precipitation--about 400 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about -3 degrees C
Depth class--very deep (shallow to permafrost)
Drainage class--poorly drained
Soil moisture regime--aquic
Soil temperature regime--gelic
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-silty, mixed, superactive, nonacid, subgelic Typic Histoturbels

TYPICAL PEDON: Nikaat mucky peat on an east facing convex, linear plain talf, on a slope of 1 percent at an elevation of 86 m (The soil was wet, non-satiated to 43 cm, moist to 50 cm, dry to 72 cm, and wet, satiated to 97 cm when described on June 24, 2018.)

 Oe/Ajj--0 to 23 cm; mucky peat and highly organic silt loam, 90 percent brown (7.5YR 5/2) dry, very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2) moist and 10 percent dark gray (10YR 4/1) dry, black (10YR 2/1) moist; nonsticky, nonplastic; many very fine roots and common fine roots; very strongly acid (pH 4.5); abrupt irregular boundary

 A/Oijj--23 to 43 cm; ashy sandy loam and peat, 85 percent brown (10YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist and 15 percent dark gray (7.5YR 4/1) dry, black (7.5YR 2.5/1) moist; weak thin platy structure; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine roots; common very fine irregular pores; strongly acid (pH 5.2); clear irregular boundary

 A/Oejj--43 to 72 cm; silt loam and mucky peat, 60 percent brown (7.5YR 5/2) dry, dark brown (7.5YR 3/2) moist and 40 percent brown (7.5YR 4/2) dry, very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2) moist; weak thin platy structure; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine roots; few fine irregular pores; strongly acid (pH 5.5); clear irregular boundary

 C/Oefjj--72 to 150 cm; permanently frozen silt loam and mucky peat, 60 percent brown (10YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist and 40 percent dark grayish brown (10YR 4/2) dry, very dark brown (10YR 2/2) moist; weak thin platy structure; very firm, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine roots; moderately acid (pH 5.6)
