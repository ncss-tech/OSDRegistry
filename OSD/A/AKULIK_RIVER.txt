AKULIK RIVER SERIES

Depth class: very deep
Drainage class: poorly drained
Parent material: loamy alluvium derived from igneous, metamorphic and sedimentary rock over loamy marine deposits
Landscape: coastal plains
Landform: delta plains
Slope: 0 to 2 percent
Mean annual precipitation: about 300 mm (12 in)
Mean annual air temperature: about -3 degrees C. (27 degrees F.)
Frost-free period: 75 to 90 days

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-loamy, mixed, superactive, nonacid, subgelic Typic Gelaquepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Akulik River fine sandy loam in a sedge and dwarf willow community on a linear, linear talf on a slope of 0.5 percent at an elevation of 3.4m (11 ft)

     Ag   --0 to 5 cm (0 to 2 in); fine sandy loam; very dark gray (10YR 3/1) broken face moist, gray (10YR 5/1) broken face dry; weak medium subangular blocky parts to weak medium granular structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; slightly fluid; many very fine, fine, and common medium roots throughout; common very fine dendritic tubular pores; mica flakes; 3.1 mS of EC; positive to alpha-alpha dipyridyl measured in matrix; noneffervescent; neutral (pH 6.7); clear smooth boundary. (2 to 10 cm, 1 to 4 in thick)

     Bg--5 to 25 cm (2 to 10 in); very fine sandy loam; very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) broken face moist, grayish brown (10YR 5/2) broken face dry; weak medium subangular blocky parts to weak fine granular structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, slightly plastic; slightly fluid; many very fine, fine, and common medium roots throughout; few very fine dendritic tubular pores; 18 percent medium prominent irregular strong brown (7.5YR 4/6) masses of oxidized iron; mica flakes; 8 mS of EC; positive to alpha-alpha dipyridyl measured in matrix; noneffervescent; moderately acid (pH 5.6); clear smooth boundary. (15 to 30 cm, 6 to 12 in thick)

     2Cg1--25 to 65 cm (10 to 26 in); very fine sandy loam; very dark gray (N 3/), broken face moist, gray (N 5/), broken face dry; massive; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; slightly fluid; many very fine roots throughout; few very fine dendritic tubular pores; mica flakes; 2.4 mS of EC; noneffervescent; moderately acid (pH 5.8); gradual boundary. (5 to 60 cm, 2 to 24 in thick)

     2Cg2--65 to 200 cm (26 to 79 in); very fine sandy loam; very dark gray (N 3/), broken face moist gray (N 5/), broken face dry;  massive; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; slightly fluid; few very fine roots throughout; many very fine irregular and few fine dendritic tubular pores; mica flakes; 3.4 mS of EC; noneffervescent; moderately acid (pH 6.0).

TYPE LOCATION: Nulato Hills - Continental Divide and Blackburn Hills Soil Survey Area, Alaska; latitude 64.8585540, longitude -160.9456090, datum WGS84, UTM north 7194108.51 and UTM east 407778.04, zone 4, datum WGS84.

COMPETING SERIES: This is the Tuksuk (AK) series.
Tuksuk soils have a paralithic contact at depths between 80 to 150 cm (32 to 60 in), have an organic surface, lack n values > 0.7, have a mean annual air temperature range of -5 to -2.5 degrees C. (23 to 24.8 degrees F.), and have a mean annual precipitation range of 597 to 696 mm (23.5 to 27.4 in)

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Elevation: 0 to 25 meters (0 to 82 feet)
Parent material: loamy alluvium derived from igneous, metamorphic and sedimentary rock over loamy marine deposits
Landform: delta plains
Slope: 0 to 2 percent
Mean annual precipitation: 300 to 400 mm (12 to 16 in)
Mean annual air temperature: -5 to -1 degrees C. (23 to 30 degrees F.)
Frost free period: 75 to 90 days

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These are the Ivgich and Ryanlake soils.
Ivgich soils have ponding, have sulfidic materials, lack a cambic horizon, have a cryic soil temperature regime, have frequent, long flooding, and occur on depressions on delta plains and abandoned channels on delta plains
Ryanlake soils have permafrost, have cryoturbation, have an organic surface thicker than 40 cm (16 in), have an isotic mineralogy class, and occur on terrace treads

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY: 
Drainage class: poorly drained
Saturation during normal years: -saturated between 0 and 25 cm (0 and 10) during the growing season
Saturated Hydraulic Conductivity: high in the Ag horizon, and moderately high to high in the Bg, 2Cg1, and 2Cg2 horizons
Permeability: moderate to rapid in the Ag, Bg, 2Cg1, and 2Cg2 horizons
Runoff: very high
Flooding: occasional, brief

USE AND VEGETATION: 
Use: wildlife habitat, recreation, and subsistence
Native vegetation: sedge, Alaska bog willow, black crowberry, nutty saw-wort, beachhead iris, American dunegrass, reedgrass

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: MLRA 240, Nulato Hills-Southern Seward Peninsula Highlands. The series is of small extent.
