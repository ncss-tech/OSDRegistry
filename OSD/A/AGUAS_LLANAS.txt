Depth Class--Very deep

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Fine-loamy, mixed, subactive, nonacid, isohyperthermic Typic Fluviwassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Aguas Llanas mucky fine sandy loam on a flood-tidal delta flat in the inland side of a barrier island complex on a coastal lagoon. (Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated).

 Aseg--0 to 14 centimeters; very dark gray (N 3/), mucky fine sandy loam; 2 percent shell fragments; structureless, massive; nonfluid; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, slight reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderately alkaline (pH 8.4), slightly alkaline (pH 7.6) oxidized pH; clear boundary.

 Cseg1--14 to 62 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (10Y 3/1), fine sandy loam; 2 percent shell fragments; structureless, massive; nonfluid; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, slight reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), slightly alkaline (pH 7.7) oxidized pH; gradual boundary.

 Cseg2--62 to 85 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (10Y 3/1), sandy clay loam; 2 percent shell fragments; structureless, massive; nonfluid; moderate sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, slight reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), slightly alkaline (pH 7.8) oxidized pH; clear boundary.

 Cg1--85 to 114 centimeters; dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1), sandy clay loam; structureless, massive, nonfluid; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, no reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), slightly alkaline (pH 7.8) oxidized pH; clear boundary.

 Cg2--114 to 168 centimeters; dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1), clay loam; structureless, massive; nonfluid; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, no reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), slightly alkaline (pH 7.8) oxidized pH; gradual boundary.

 2Cg3--168 to 226 centimeters; dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1), sandy loam; structureless, massive, nonfluid; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, no reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), slightly alkaline (pH 7.8) oxidized pH.

TYPE LOCATION: Guayama municipality, Puerto Rico; Jobos Bay Lagoon, approximately 3.4 kilometers (2.1 miles) south-southeast from the Jobos Bay National Estuary Research Reserve (JBNERR) central building and approximately 0.85 kilometers (0.42 miles) northeast of Cayo Caribe's southernmost shore point.

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Parent Material--Fine-loamy lagoonal deposits
Landscape--Coastal lagoons in semiarid coastal plain, Puerto Rico
Landform--Flood-tidal delta flat
Mean Annual Air Temperature--22 to 31 degrees C (72 to 88 degrees F)
Mean Annual Precipitation--508 to 1,145 millimeters (20 to 45 inches)
Mean Annual Water Temperature--26 to 32 degrees C (79 to 90 degrees F)
Water salinity range--28 to 43 parts per thousand

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Bahia Jobos and Caricoche soils.
Bahia Jobos soils--occur on similar landscape positions but more commonly on the flood-tidal delta slopes which are slightly lower on the landscape and further from the barrier islands and reefs. The Bahia Jobos soils also have coarser textures
Caricoche soils--are loamy-skeletal (dominated by reef fragments) and occur on the adjacent flood-tidal delta channels that transport materials onto the flat and slope landforms

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous (permanently submersed)
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately low to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic 
Soil is continuously inundated by saline water

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major Uses--Used for wildlife habitat, recreational boating, fishing, and swimming, marinas, mooring fields, and commercial shellfish habitat.  These soils provide improved water quality and flood / storm surge water storage.
Dominant Vegetation--Turtle grass (Thalasia testudinum), Paddle grass (Halofila decipiens), Syringodium filiforme (manatee grass) Brush seaweed (Penicillus capitatus), and watercress alga (Halimeda opuntia) plants.

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: 
General area--Coastal lagoons along the southern coastal plain of Puerto Rico.

SOIL SURVEY REGIONAL OFFICE (SSRO) RESPONSIBLE: Special Projects Office
SERIES ESTABLISHED: Guayama Municipality, Puerto Rico; 2024. The name means shallow waters due to it occurring on the shallow protected areas behind the barrier islands south of the Jobos Bay lagoon, where it was originally recognized.

REMARKS: This subaqueous series is mapped in areas previously published as water in Puerto Rico Soil Survey publications.

Diagnostic horizons and features recognized in this pedon-
Peraquic soil moisture regime--Positive soil water potential (permanently submerged) at the soil surface. (under 0 to 2 meters of salt water).
Ocrhic epipedon--the zone 0 to 14 centimeters (0 to 5 inches).
Oxidized pH values--the shell fragments and Halimeda materials that occur throughout this soil buffer the oxidized pH preventing potential hypersulfidic materials from developing
Haplic feature--soil in nonfluid at least in some layer from 20 to 50 centimeters.
