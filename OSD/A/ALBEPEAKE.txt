TYPICAL PEDON: Albepeake loamy sand in a dredge-deposit shoal under 1.2 meters of brackish water (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 ^Aseg--0 to 12 centimeters; 60 percent black (N 2.5/) and 40 percent greenish gray (10Y 5/1) loamy sand; structureless, single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; slightly fluid; common medium and common fine roots throughout; no sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.6 dS/m); neutral (pH 7.0); clear boundary.

 ^Cseg1--12 to 34 centimeters; dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1) loamy sand; structureless, single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; few fine roots throughout; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; no effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.7 dS/m); neutral (pH 6.8); abrupt boundary.

 ^Cseg2--34 to 73 centimeters; very dark gray (N 3/) silt loam; structureless, massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; very fluid; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.7 dS/m); neutral (pH 7.0); clear boundary.

 ^Cseg3--73 to 97 centimeters; dark gray (N 4/) silt loam; structureless, massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; very fluid; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderate effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.8 dS/m); neutral (pH 7.2); abrupt boundary.

 ^Cseg4--97 to 122 centimeters; dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1) fine sandy loam; structureless, massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; slightly fluid; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderate effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (1.0 dS/m); neutral (pH 7.2); clear boundary.

 Cseg5--122 to 205 centimeters; dark gray (N 4/) silty clay loam; structureless, massive; moderately sticky, moderately plastic; very fluid; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (1.3 dS/m); slightly alkaline (pH 7.8).

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Kill Devil, Lost, and Princess Anne soils.
Kill Devil soils--are on low energy lagoon bottoms, fluviomarine bottoms, and mainland coves and formed in fine-loamy estuarine deposits.
Kitty Hawk soils--are on washover-fans and contain hypersulfidic materials.
Princess Anne soils--are in estuarine tidal streams, typically have O horizons, and contain hypersulfidic materials.

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately low to high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish water. The water level is primarily driven by wind tides.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--The area is used as a spoil pile for dredge materials from the Intracoastal Waterway.
Typical vegetation--Vegetation may include widgeon grass (Ruppia maritima), sago pondweed (Stuckenia pectinata), and Eurasian milfoil (Myriophyllum spicatum).

REMARKS:
The series name is a combination of words derived from "Albemarle and Chesapeake Canal". The Canal connects two major watersheds on the east coast, the Albemarle-Pamlico estuary and the Chesapeake Bay estuary. Construction of the Albemarle and Chesapeake Canal was completed in 1859 and is now a part of the Intracoastal Waterway, which is adjacent to the type location for the series. Albepeake soils were previously mapped as water in North Carolina and Virginia soil surveys.

Diagnostic horizons and other diagnostic soil characteristics recognized in this pedon are (Keys to Soil Taxonomy, Thirteenth Edition, 2022):
Human-transported materials--The zone from 0 to 122 centimeters formed in human-transported materials.
Fluidity--The zone from 0 to 12 centimeters and 34 to 205 centimeters has a fluidity class of slightly fluid to very fluid.
Peraquic feature--Zero or positive soil water potential at the soil surface (permanently submerged).
