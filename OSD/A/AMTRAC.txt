GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Apalachicola, Dry Bar, Eastpoint, McKissack, Pelican Reef, Sheepshead, and Thalinectes soils.
Apalachicola soils--Are on bay and lagoon bottoms and formed in fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Dry Bar soils--Are on shoals and reefs and formed in sandy-skeletal lagoonal deposits.
Eastpoint soils--Are on submerged wave-cut platforms and have coarser sandy textures throughout.
McKissack soils--Are in estuarine tidal streams, formed in sandy estuarine deposits and often have chroma of 3.
Pelican Reef soils--Are on relict flood-tidal deltas and formed in sandy lagoonal deposits.
Sheepshead soils--Are on barrier coves and are sandy throughout.
Thalinectes soils--Are on washover-fans and longshore bars and are sandy throughout. 

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--High to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by salt water.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--In 0 to 2 meter water depths, these soils are used for Northern hard-shell clam (Mercenaria mercenaria) and, to a lesser extent, Eastern oyster (Crassostrea virginica) cultivation and production. Non-cultivated areas also support native Southern Quahog clam (Mercenaria campechiensis).  
Typical vegetation--Vegetation is largely absent on Amtrac soils managed for aquaculture. Amtrac soils in a natural state support turtle grass (Thalassia testudinum), widgeon grass (Ruppia maritima), and some shoal grass (Halodule wrightii).
