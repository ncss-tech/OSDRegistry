ASULU SERIES

Landscape--valley
Landform--loess covered terraces and plains
Microfeature--polygon
Slope--0 to 2 percent
Parent material--cryoturbated organic material over cryoturbated loess 
Mean annual precipitation--about 450 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about -3 degrees C
Depth class--very deep (moderately deep to permafrost)  
Drainage class--poorly drained
Soil moisture regime--aquic
Soil temperature regime--gelic
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy, mixed, dysic, subgelic Terric Hemistels

TYPICAL PEDON: Asulu mucky peat on an east by north-east facing convex, linear plain talf, on a slope of 2 percent at an elevation of 16 m (The soil was wet, non-satiated to 15 cm and wet, satiated to 150 cm when described on May 30, 2018.)

 Oi/Oajj  --0 to 15 cm; peat, 97 percent reddish yellow (7.5YR 7/6) dry, strong brown (7.5YR 5/6) moist, and 3 percent gray (10YR 5/1) dry, black (10YR 2/1) moist; many very fine and common medium and fine roots; extremely acid (pH 3.9); abrupt wavy boundary

 Oe1--15 to 35 cm; mucky peat, reddish yellow (7.5YR 6/6) dry, strong brown (7.5YR 4/6) moist; many very fine and common medium and fine roots; very strongly acid (pH 4.6); clear wavy boundary

 Oe2--35 to 42 cm; mucky peat, light brown (7.5YR 6/4) dry, brown (7.5YR 4/4) moist; many very fine roots and common fine roots; extremely acid (pH 4.4) wavy boundary

 C/Oajj--42 to 60 cm; silt loam, 97 percent light brownish gray (10YR 6/2) dry, dark grayish brown (10YR 4/2) moist, and 3 percent dark gray (10YR 4/1) dry, black (10YR 2/1) moist; few very fine irregular pores; massive; extremely firm, nonsticky, nonplastic; ; very strongly acid (pH 4.9)

 C/Oajjf--60 to 150 cm; permanently frozen silt loam, 97 percent light brownish gray (10YR 6/2) dry, dark grayish brown (10YR 4/2) moist, and 3 percent dark gray (10YR 4/1) dry, black (10YR 2/1) moist; massive; extremely firm, nonsticky, nonplastic; ; very strongly acid (pH 4.9)
