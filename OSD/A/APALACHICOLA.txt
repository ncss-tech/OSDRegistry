COMPETING SERIES:
These are the Cash and Slough series.
Cash soils--formed on mainland coves, often support sub-aquatic vegetation and have higher soil organic carbon content in the surface horizon and 1:5 electrical conductivity between 1.5 and 4.0 dS/m.
Slough soils--formed on estuarine tidal streams and have 1:5 electrical conductivity between 0.6 and 3.0 dS/m.

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Cash, Dregs, Dry Bar, Eastpoint, Lighter, Norman, Pelican Reef, Sandbank, Sandfly, Sikes, Slough, and Thalinectes soils.
Dregs soils--Are on dredge-deposit shoals and formed in sandy human-transported materials.
Dry Bar soils--Are on shoals and oyster reefs, formed in sandy lagoonal deposits, and have sandy-skeletal particle size class.
Eastpoint soils--Are on submerged wave-cut platforms on the mainland side and formed in sandy lagoonal and estuarine deposits.
Lighter soils--Are in dredge channels and formed in sandy lagoonal deposits with sulfidic materials occurring within the top 100 centimeters.
Norman soils--Are on shoals and oyster reefs, formed in fine-loamy lagoonal deposits, and have a loamy-skeletal particle size class.
Pelican Reef soils--Are on relict flood-tidal deltas, formed in sandy lagoonal deposits, and support limited subaquatic vegetation.
Sandbank soils--Are on fluviomarine bottoms, formed in sandy estuarine materials and have mica flakes in some part of the profile.
Sandfly soils--Are in estuarine tidal streams, formed in coarse-loamy lagoonal over fluviomarine deposits, and contain 20 centimeters or more of buried A horizons.
Sikes soils--Are on flood-tidal deltas and formed in sandy lagoonal deposits. 
Thalinectes soils--Are on washover-fan flats and slopes, formed in sandy lagoonal and marine materials, and support significant stands of subaquatic vegetation.

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately high to high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish or salt water.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--The area is used for recreational fishing, hunting, and boating, commercial fishing and limited oyster production.
Typical vegetation--Typically absent in deeper areas with turbid water, limited light penetration, and highly variable salinity throughout the water column. Where low turbidity exists, shoal grass (Ruppia maritima) may establish limited stands.

REMARKS:
Typical Pedon Taxonomic Features:
Ochric epipedon--The zone from 0 to 15 centimeters
Reduced matrix--The zone from 0 to 43 centimeters and 91 to 210 centimeters has a soil color matrix with a chroma of 2 or less
Hyposulfidic materials--The zone from 0 to 199 centimeters has an initial pH that decreases by 1 or more units but remains at a value of 4.0 or more after 16 weeks of moist aerobic incubation
Fluidity--Very fluid from 0 to 199 centimeters; moderately fluid below
Peraquic feature--Zero or positive soil water potential at the soil surface (permanently submerged)
Taxonomic Version: Keys to Soil Taxonomy, Thirteenth Edition, 2022
Other: Named after Apalachicola Bay, Florida where it was first observed. Apalachicola soils were previously mapped as water in Florida soil surveys.
