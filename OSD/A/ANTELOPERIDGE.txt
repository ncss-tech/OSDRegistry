ANTELOPERIDGE SERIES

Landscape--hills
Landform--hillslopes
Slope--1 to 40 percent
Parent material-colluvium derived from obsidian and rhyolite
Mean annual precipitation--about 475 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 3 degrees C
Depth class--very deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--xeric
Soil temperature regime--cryic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-loamy over sandy or sandy-skeletal, mixed, superactive Xeric Haplocryolls

TYPICAL PEDON:  Anteloperidge sandy loam in an area of rangeland (Colors are for dry soil unless otherwise noted.)

 A1--0 to 8 cm; brown (10YR 4/3) sandy loam, very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) moist; weak fine subangular blocky structure parting to weak fine granular; soft, very friable, nonsticky and slightly plastic; common very fine and fine and few medium roots; few fine tubular pores; 5 percent gravel; neutral (pH 6.8); clear wavy boundary.

 A2--8 to 25 cm; dark brown (10YR 3/3) sandy loam, very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) moist; weak medium and fine subangular blocky structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and slightly plastic; common very fine and fine and few medium roots; few very fine tubular pores; 5 percent gravel; neutral (pH 6.8); abrupt wavy boundary.

 Bw1--25 to 48 cm; light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) sandy loam, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure parting to weak fine subangular blocky; soft, friable, nonsticky and slightly plastic; few very fine, fine, and medium roots; few very fine tubular pores; 10 percent gravel; neutral (pH 7.2); clear wavy boundary.

 Bw2--48 to 75 cm; yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) gravelly coarse sandy loam, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure parting to weak fine subangular blocky; soft, very friable, nonsticky and slightly plastic; few very fine and fine roots; few fine tubular pores; 15 percent gravel; slightly alkaline (pH 7.4); clear wavy boundary.

 2CB--75 to 107 cm; light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) gravelly sand, 70 percent black (7.5YR 2.5/1) and 30 percent dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak fine subangular blocky structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; few very fine, fine, and medium roots; few very fine tubular pores; 15 percent gravel; slightly alkaline (pH 7.4); gradual wavy boundary.

 2C1--107 to 135 cm; grayish brown (10YR 5/2) gravelly sand, 60 percent black (7.5YR 2.5/1) and 40 percent dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; single grain; loose, nonsticky and nonplastic; many fine interstitial pores; 20 percent gravel and 5 percent cobbles; slightly alkaline (pH 7.6); clear smooth boundary.

 2C2--135 to 150 cm; grayish brown (10YR 5/2) very gravelly sand, 60 percent black (7.5YR 2.5/1) and 40 percent dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; single grain; loose, nonsticky and nonplastic; many fine interstitial pores; 30 percent gravel and 15 percent cobbles; slightly alkaline (pH 7.6).

TYPE LOCATION:  Clark County, Idaho, about 4.4 km north of Kilgore, Idaho; about 188 m east and 404 m south of the northwest corner of section 24, T. 13 N., R. 39 E.; Antelope Valley U.S. Geological Survey quadrangle; latitude 44.442 degrees north, longitude 111.81742 degrees west, datum WGS 84 (coordinates determined with a GPS)
