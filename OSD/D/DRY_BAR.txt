DRY BAR SERIES

MLRA(s)--152A
Depth class--Very deep
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Water depth range--0 to 3 meters (0 to 10 feet)
Parent material--Sandy-skeletal lagoonal deposits
Geomorphic location--Shoals and reefs
Slope range--0 to 1 percent
Mean annual air temperature--21 degrees C (70 degrees F)
Mean annual water temperature--24 degrees C (75 degrees F)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Sandy-skeletal, siliceous, hyperthermic Typic Fluviwassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Dry Bar very shelly loamy sand on a shoal under 1.6 meters of salt water (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 Cseg1--0 to 28 centimeters; 50 percent dark gray (2.5Y 4/1) and 50 percent very dark gray (2.5Y 3/1) very shelly loamy sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 40 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderate effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.0 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), strongly alkaline (pH 8.5) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg2--28 to 114 centimeters; grayish brown (10YR 5/2) very shelly loamy sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 50 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.9 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.4), strongly alkaline (pH 8.5) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg3--114 to 200 centimeters; gray (2.5Y 5/1) loamy fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 8 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.8 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), strongly alkaline (pH 8.6) oxidized pH

TYPE LOCATION: Franklin County, Florida; Apalachicola Bay; approximately 1.1 kilometers (0.7 miles) north of the intersection of Akel Street and Marks Street, approximately 7.6 kilometers (4.7 miles) south of the intersection of South Bay Shore Drive and Highway 98, and 2.7 kilometers (1.7 miles) west of the terminus of Austin Street on St. George Island; USGS topographic quadrangle: Apalachicola, FL.

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Landscape--Bays and lagoons along the Gulf Coast
Landform--Shoals and reefs

Water temperature--18 to 28 degrees C (64 to 82 degrees F)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Apalachicola, Eastpoint, Lighthouse, Pelican Reef, and Thalincetes soils.
Apalachicola soils--Formed on bay and lagoon bottoms from fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Eastpoint soils--Formed on submerged wave-cut platforms from sandy lagoonal deposits.
Lighthouse soils--Formed on shoals and reefs from loamy-skeletal lagoonal deposits.
Pelican Reef soils--Formed on relict flood-tidal deltas from sandy lagoonal deposits.
Thalinectes soils--Formed on longshore bars and washover-fans from sandy lagoonal deposits. 

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--High to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish or salt water

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--Commercial uses include production and harvesting Eastern oysters (Crassostrea virginica). Recreational uses include oyster harvesting and fishing.
Typical vegetation--Vegetation is rarely observed on Dry Bar soils due to the prevalence of active and inactive oyster shell beds occurring on the surface.      

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT:
General area--Brackish and saltwater shoals and reefs along the Gulf.
Land Resource Regions--T - Atlantic and Gulf Coast Lowland Forest and Crop Region

REMARKS:
Typical Pedon Taxonomic Features:
Reduced matrix--The zone from 0 to 200 centimeters has a soil color matrix with a chroma of 2 or less.
Peraquic feature--Zero or positive soil water potential at the soil surface (permanently submerged).
Taxonomic Version: Keys to Soil Taxonomy, Thirteenth Edition, 2022
Other: The series is named after Dry Bar, a historic oyster reef on the east side of St. Vincent Island, where first observed. Dry Bar soils were previously mapped as water in Florida soil survey publications.
