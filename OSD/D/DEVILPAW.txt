DEVILPAW SERIES

Depth class: Deep
Drainage class: Well drained
Parent material: herbaceous organic material over sandy and loamy volcanic ash over medial silty volcanic ash over sandy and gravelly till
Landscape: Islands 
Landform: Hills and plains
Slopes: 2 to 20 percent
Mean annual precipitation: about 2250 mm (88.5 in)
Mean annual air temperature: about 2 degrees C. (34 degrees F.)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Ashy over loamy-skeletal, glassy over mixed, superactive Spodic Vitricryands

TYPICAL PEDON: Devilpaw ashy silt loam - on a cleared spruce plains at 16 meters (54 feet) elevation. (All colors are for moist soil)

 Oe--0 to 8 centimeters (0 to 3 inches); very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2), dark brown (7.5YR 3/2), dry; moderately decomposed plant material; very friable, soft, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine, fine, medium, and few coarse roots throughout; extremely acid (pH 4.4); abrupt smooth boundary. (2 to 13 cm, 1 to 5 in thick)

 C1--8 to 15 centimeters (3 to 6 inches); pale brown (10YR 6/3), very pale brown (10YR 7/3), dry; ashy silt loam; weak medium platy structure; very friable, soft, nonsticky, nonplastic; strongly acid (pH 5.1); clear smooth boundary. 

 C2--15 to 29 centimeters (6 to 11 inches); ashy very fine sandy loam; weak thin platy structure; very friable, soft, nonsticky, nonplastic; common fine roots throughout; strongly acid (pH 5.4); clear smooth boundary. 

 C3--29 to 50 centimeters (11 to 20 inches); light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) ashy sandy loam; massive; very friable, soft, nonsticky, nonplastic; common fine and medium roots throughout; moderately acid (pH 5.8); clear smooth boundary. 

 C4--50 to 65 centimeters (20 to 26 inches); light yellowish brown (2.5Y 6/4) ashy loamy sand; massive; loose, nonsticky, nonplastic; common fine and medium roots throughout; moderately acid, (pH 5.9); abrupt wavy boundary. (Combined thickness is 41 to 76 cm, 16 to 30 in thick)

 2A--65 to 68 centimeters (26 to 27 inches); black (7.5YR 2.5/1) medial silt loam; massive; very friable, soft, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine, fine, and medium roots throughout; strongly acid (pH 5.4); clear wavy boundary. (0 to 8 cm, 0 to 3 in thick)

 2Bhs--68 to 87 centimeters (27 to 34 inches); gravelly highly organic medial silt loam; weak fine subangular blocky structure; friable, soft, nonsticky, nonplastic; common fine and medium roots throughout; 30 percent gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.5); clear wavy boundary. (10 to 30 cm, 4 to 12 in thick)

 3C--87 to 200 centimeters (34 to 79 inches); dark grayish brown (2.5Y 4/2) very gravelly sandy loam; massive; friable, slightly hard, slightly sticky, nonplastic; 50 percent gravel and 10 percent cobble; moderately acid (pH 5.6). 

TYPE LOCATION: Kodiak Archipelago Soil Survey Area, Alaska; Latitude 57 degrees 54 minutes 36.00 seconds and Longitude 153 degrees 29 minutes 58.92 seconds west, UTM north 6422139.61 and UTM east 470413.90, zone 5, Datum WGS84.

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:

Landform: Hills and plains
Slopes: 2 to 20 percent
Mean annual precipitation: 1500 to 3000 mm (59 to 118 in)
Mean annual temperature: 0 to 4 degrees C. (32 to 39 degrees F.)
Frost free season: 20 to 50 days

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These are the Izhut, Kazakof, Kodiak, Marka, Missak, Port Lions, and Tonki series. 
Izhut soils lack over thickened volcanic ashy horizons, have aquic features within 41 to 100 cm (16 to 40 in) and occur on similar landforms in drainage ways and swales.
Kazakof soils lack over thickened volcanic ashy horizons, have lithic contact within 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in) and occur on similar landform positions.
Kodiak soils lack over thickened volcanic ashy horizons and occur on similar landform positions.
Marka soils lack over thickened volcanic ashy horizons, have lithic contact within 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in) and occur on similar landform positions.
Port Lions soils lack over thickened volcanic ashy horizons, have lithic contact within 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in) and occur on similar landform positions.
Tonki soils lack over thickened volcanic ashy horizons, have aquic features within 0 to 25 cm (0 to 10 in) and occur on similar landforms in depression, drainage ways, and swales.

DRAINAGE AND PERMEABILITY: 
Drainage class: well drained
Saturated Hydraulic Conductivity: moderately high to very high in the C horizons, moderately high to high in the 2A and 2Bhs horizons, and high to very high in the 3C.
Permeability: moderate to very rapid in the C horizon, moderate to rapid in the 2A and 2Bhs, and 3C horizons.
Runoff: very low to low.
