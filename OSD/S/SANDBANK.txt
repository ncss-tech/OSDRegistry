TYPICAL PEDON: Sandbank fine sand on a fluviomarine bottom under 0.3 meters of brackish water. (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 Cseg1--0 to 41 centimeters; black (N 2.5/) fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 5 percent very fine mixed mica flakes throughout; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; strong effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.7 dS/m); strongly alkaline (pH 8.5), very strongly acid (pH 4.7) oxidized pH; abrupt boundary

 Cseg2--41 to 132 centimeters; 90 precent olive gray (5Y 4/2) and 5 percent black (N 2.5/) and 5 percent dark gray (5Y 4/1) fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; few very fine and coarse roots throughout; 5 percent very fine mixed mica flakes throughout; 2 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderate effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.6 dS/m); strongly alkaline (pH 8.7), strongly acid (pH 5.2) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg3--132 to 200 centimeters; 60 percent very dark gray (2.5Y 3/1) and 30 percent gray (2.5Y 5/1) and 10 percent dark gray (2.5Y 4/1) fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; few fine roots throughout; 5 percent very fine mixed mica flakes throughout; 2 percent shell fragments; strong sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderate effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.4 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 8.4), extremely acid (pH 3.5) oxidized pH; thin lenses of sapric soil material at the bottom of the horizon

COMPETING SERIES:
These are the Broadaxe, Dregs, Eastpoint, Lutz, McKissack, Mullet, Pelican Reef, and Thalinectes series.
Broadaxe soils--Formed on submerged wave-cut platforms and contain buried spodic horizons.
Dregs soils--Formed on dredge-deposit shoals from sandy human transported material.
Eastpoint soils--Formed on submerged wave-cut platforms from sandy lagoonal deposits and do not have mica present throughout the profile.
Lutz soils--Formed on flood-tidal deltas from sandy lagoonal deposits, do not have mica present throughout the profile, and do not have oxidized pHs below 7.8.
McKissack soils--Formed on brackish estuarine tidal streams from eroded spodic soil materials, have between 6 to 12 kg m-2 organic carbon from 0 to 100 centimeters and organic stains between sand grains. 
Mullet soils--Formed on washover-fan flats and do not have mica present throughout the profile.
Pelican Reef soils--Formed on relict flood-tidal deltas from sandy lagoonal deposits, do not have mica present through the profile.
Thalinectes soils--Formed in sandy lagoonal and marine deposits on washover-fans and longshore bars and have higher soil organic carbon densities. 

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Landscape--Bays and Lagoons along the Gulf Coast

Parent material--Sandy estuarine deposits 
Slope--0 to 2 percent
Water depth range--0 to 2 meters (0 to 6 feet) below mean sea level
Water salinity range--5 to 20 parts per thousand
Water temperature range--18 to 30 degrees C (64 to 86 degrees F)
Soil temperature range--19 to 27 degrees C (66 to 81 degrees F)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Apalachicola, Cash, and Slough soils.
Apalachicola soils--Are on bay and lagoon bottoms and formed in fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Cash soils--Are on mainland coves and formed in fine-loamy estuarine deposits.
Slough soils--Are on brackish estuarine tidal streams and formed in fine-loamy estuarine deposits.

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--High to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish or salt water.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--The area is used for recreational fishing, hunting, and boating. Some areas may support limited aquatic habitat.
Typical vegetation--Vegetation presence is largely determined by energy of depositional flow. In areas where turbidity is low, tape grass (Vallisneria spiralis) may establish or species of brown algae.

REMARKS:
Typical Pedon Taxonomic Features:
Reduced matrix--The zone from 0 to 200 centimeters has a soil color matrix chroma of 2 or less
Hyposulfidic materials--The zone from 0 to 132 centimeters has an initial pH that decreases by 1 or more units but remains at a value of 4.0 or more after 16 weeks of moist aerobic incubation
Hypersulfidic materials--The zone from 132 to 200 centimeters has an initial pH that decreased by 1 or more units and is less than 4.0 after 16 weeks of moist aerobic incubation
Peraquic feature--Zero or positive soil water potential at the soil surface (permanently submerged)
Taxonomic Version: Keys to Soil Taxonomy, Thirteenth Edition, 2022
Other: Named after sand banks, which are depositional, levee like features in river deltas. Sandbank soils were previously mapped as water in Florida soil surveys.
