SWAN SERIES

Depth class: Very deep
Drainage class: Very poorly drained
Flooding frequency and duration: Very frequent, brief
Saturated hydraulic conductivity: Low to moderately low in the surface and moderately high to very high throughout the rest of the profile
Parent material: Fine-loamy alluvium
Slope: 0 to 1 percent
Mean annual air temperature: 22 degrees C (72 degrees F)
Mean annual precipitation: 1067 millimeters (42 inches)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Fine-loamy, mixed, superactive, calcareous, hyperthermic Typic Endoaquolls

TYPICAL PEDON: Swan mucky peat on a nearly level flood plain at an elevation of 2 meters. (Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated.)

 Oe--0 to 8 centimeters; dark olive gray (5Y 3/2) mucky peat, olive gray (5Y 4/2) dry; 60 percent unrubbed fiber content, 30 percent rubbed fiber content; H4 von Post humification; massive; soft, very friable; many fine and medium roots; strongly saline; moderately alkaline (pH 8.2); clear smooth boundary. (Oe horizon is 0 to 13 centimeters thick)

 Ag--8 to 38 centimeters; very dark gray (N 3/) clay, dark gray (N 4/) dry; weak fine prismatic structure parting to moderate fine angular blocky structure; very hard, firm; few medium and common fine roots; many fine faint black (N 2.5/) iron depletions; 1 percent fine fragments of shells; strongly effervescent (HCl); strongly saline; moderately alkaline (pH 8.2); clear wavy boundary. (Ag horizon is 25 to 43 centimeters thick)

 Cg1--38 to 76 centimeters; dark gray (5Y 4/1) sandy clay loam, gray (5Y 5/1) dry; massive; hard, friable; few fine roots; 1 percent fine shell fragments; strongly effervescent (HCl); strongly saline; moderately alkaline (pH 8.2); clear wavy boundary.

 Cg2--76 to 122 centimeters; gray (5Y 6/1) sandy loam, light gray (5Y7/1) dry; massive; soft, very friable; few fine roots; 10 percent coarse distinct very dark gray (5Y 3/1) and dark gray (5Y 4/1) iron depletions; 1 percent fine shell fragments; strongly effervescent (HCl); strongly saline; moderately alkaline (pH 8.2); clear wavy boundary.

 Cg3--122 to 160 centimeters; light brownish gray (10YR 6/2) loamy sand, light gray (10YR 7/2) dry; single grain; soft, loose; 20 percent coarse prominent gray (5Y 5/1) and 10 percent coarse prominent olive (5Y 5/3) and 1 percent medium prominent grayish green (5G 5/2) iron depletions; 1 percent fine shell fragments; strongly effervescent (HCl); strongly saline; moderately alkaline (pH 8.2); clear wavy boundary.

 Cg4--160 to 200 centimeters; olive gray (5Y 5/2) loamy sand, light olive gray (5Y 6/2) dry, single grain; slightly hard, loose; 20 percent faint gray (5Y 5/1) iron depletions; 1 percent fine shell fragments; strongly effervescent (HCl); strongly saline; moderately alkaline (pH 8.2).

TYPE LOCATION: Jackson County, Texas; approximately 8.64 kilometers (5.4 miles) south-southeast from the intersection of FM 234 and FM 616 in Vanderbilt, Texas; and approximately 6.26 kilometers (3.9 miles) northwest of the intersection of Bayside Lane and FM 1593 near Formosa Plastics Corporation plant; USGS quadrangle: Point Comfort, Texas

COMPETING SERIES: There are no other series in this family. Series in similar families include Placedo and Surfside series.
Placedo soils: have a fine particle-size control section and have ochric epipedon.
Surfside soils: have a very-fine particle-size control section and a water table that fluctuates throughout the year.

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: 
These are the Aransas, Navidad, and Placedo (mentioned above) series.
Aransas soils: are on flood plains, have slickensides, and a fine smectitic particle-size control section.
Navidad soils: are on flood plains, on slightly higher landscape positions, and have thick mollic epipedons.
