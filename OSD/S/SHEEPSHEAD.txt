TYPICAL PEDON: Sheepshead shelly sandy loam in a barrier cove under 1.3 meters of salt water. (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 Cseg4--122 to 172 centimeters; 90 percent very dark greenish gray (10Y 3/1) and 10 percent dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1) loamy fine sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 5 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (2.8 dS/m); slightly alkaline (pH 7.5), slightly alkaline (pH 7.5) oxidized pH; abrupt boundary

COMPETING SERIES:
These are the Buttonwood, Endeavor, and Tea Hammock series
Buttonwood soils--Formed in barrier coves and contain submerged spodic horizons as lithologic discontinuities
Endeavor soils--Formed on relict flood-tidal delta flats and slopes with some horizons containing greater than 30 percent shell fragments and have mixed mineralogy
Tea Hammock soils--Formed in sandy dredge materials on dredge-deposit shoals

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Amtrac, Apalachicola, Dry Bar, Pelican Reef, and Thalinectes soils.
Amtrac soils--Are on high-energy lagoon bottoms and barrier coves and formed in coarse-loamy lagoon deposits.
Apalachicola soils--Are on bay and lagoon bottoms and formed in fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Dry Bar soils--Are on shoals and reefs and formed in sandy-skeletal lagoonal deposits. 
Pelican Reef soils--Are on relict flood-tidal deltas and formed in sandy lagoonal deposits.
Thalinectes soils--Are on washover-fans and longshore bars and formed in sandy lagoonal deposits. 

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--High to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by salt water.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--These soils are used for Northern hard-shell clam (Mercenaria mercenaria) and, to a lesser extent, Eastern oyster (Crassostrea virginica) cultivation and production. Non-cultivated areas also support native Southern Quahog clam (Mercenaria campechiensis).  
Typical vegetation--Vegetation is largely absent on Sheepshead soils managed for aquaculture. Sheepshead soils in a natural state support turtle grass (Thalassia testudinum), widgeon grass (Ruppia maritima), and some shoal grass (Halodule wrightii).
