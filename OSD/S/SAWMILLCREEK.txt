SAWMILLCREEK SERIES

Depth class: Very deep
Drainage class: well drained
Parent material: loess over calcareous glacial outwash
Landform: alluvial fans
Slopes: 0 to 2 percent
Mean annual precipitation: about 12 inches, 305 mm
Mean annual temperature: about 29 degrees F., -1.6 C.

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-loamy over sandy or sandy-skeletal, mixed, superactive Typic Haplocryepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Sawmillcreek silt loam - on a slope of 5 percent under grass pasture at 1200 feet elevation. (All colors are for moist soil unless noted)

	Ap--0 to 7 inches (0 to 17 cm); dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) silt loam; weak medium platy structure parting to weak fine granular structure; friable; nonsticky; non-plastic; many fine and very fine roots; few medium roots; slightly acid (pH 6.5); clear wavy boundary.

	Bw--7 to 11 inches (17 to 26 cm); brown (7.5YR 4/4) silt loam: weak medium platy structure; friable; nonsticky and nonplastic; 1 percent gravels; common fine and very fine roots; slightly acid (pH 6.5); clear smooth boundary.

	2Bw--11 to 15 inches (26 to 38 cm); yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) fine sandy loam; weak medium subangular blocky structure; friable; nonsticky and nonplastic; 1 percent gravel; common fine and very fine roots; slightly acid (pH 6.5); clear smooth boundary. (Thickness of this horizon ranges from 0 to 5 inches)

	3C1--15 to 19 inches (38 to 47 cm); dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) extremely gravelly loamy coarse sand; single grain; loose; nonsticky and nonplastic; 80 percent gravel; many fine and very fine roots; slightly effervescent; slightly alkaline (pH 7.5); clear wavy boundary.

	3Ck--19 to 60 inches (47 to 152 cm); dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) extremely gravelly loamy coarse sand; single grain; loose; nonsticky and nonplastic; 85 percent gravel; 2 percent cobbles; many fine and very fine roots; many carbonate coats and nodules on the underside of coarse fragments; violently effervescent; moderately alkaline (pH 8.0).

TYPE LOCATION: The Greater Delta Soil Survey Area, Alaska; about 20 miles 
southwest of Delta Junction; in the NW 1/4 of the NE 1/4, section 34, T. 11 S., 
R. 13E; Mt. Hayes D-3 1:63,360 quad; Fairbanks Meridian; UTM north 7089944 and 
UTM east 589602, zone 6.

COMPETING SERIES: These are the Lupine, and Maclaren series. Lupine and 
Maclaren series do not contain free carbonates.

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Parent material: loess over calcareous glacial outwash
Landform: alluvial fans
Slopes: 0 to 2 percent
Mean annual temperature: 28 to 30 degrees F., - 2.2 to 1.1 C.
Mean annual precipitation: 8 to 14 inches, 203 cm to 356 mm

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These include the Progress, Gerstle, Nenana, Volkmar, and Moosehead. All these soils occur on similar landscapes.  Progress, Gerstle, Nenana, Volkmar and Moosehead do not contain free carbonates.  
Gerstle, Volkmar, and Moosehead soils are moderately well drained. Progress, Gerstle, and Volkmar do not have coarse fragments above 40 inches.  

DRAINAGE AND PERMEABILITY: Well drained, low runoff, Saturated hydraulic conductivity is moderately high in the loamy mantle and high in the glacial outwash.

USE AND VEGETATION: Used for farmland, pasture, hay and small grains.  The native vegetation includes white spruce, black spruce, birch and aspen.

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: MLRA 229, Interior Alaska Lowlands, Alaska. The series is of minor extent.

SOIL SURVEY REGIONAL OFFICE (SSRO) RESPONSIBLE: WASILLA, ALASKA

SERIES ESTABLISHED: The Greater Nenana Soil Survey, Alaska, 2005
