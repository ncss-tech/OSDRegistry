TAXONOMIC CLASS: Sandy, mixed Folistic Dystrocryepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Sprucecreek silt loam on a west facing linear, linear tread, on a slope of 1 percent at an elevation of 146 m (The soil was moist throughout the profile when described on June 9, 2019.)

 Oi--0 to 9 cm; slightly decomposed plant material, reddish yellow (7.5YR 7/6) dry, strong brown (7.5YR 5/6) moist; many very fine, fine, and medium, and common coarse roots; very strongly acid (pH 4.5); abrupt wavy boundary

 Oa--9 to 18 cm; highly decomposed plant material, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2) moist; many very fine and common medium, fine, and coarse roots; extremely acid (pH 3.9); abrupt wavy boundary

 A--18 to 28 cm; silt loam, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) moist; weak medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; many very fine and common fine and coarse roots; 10 percent rounded gravel by volume; strongly acid (pH 5.1); clear wavy boundary

 Bw--28 to 47 cm; silt loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) dry, dark brown (7.5YR 3/4) moist; weak medium subangular blocky structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; common very fine, fine, and coarse roots; 5 percent rounded gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.1); abrupt smooth boundary

 2C--47 to 150 cm; very gravelly coarse sand, light gray (2.5Y 7/2) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; common very fine and fine roots; 10 percent rounded fine and medium gravel, 20 percent rounded coarse gravel; very strongly acid (pH 5.0)
