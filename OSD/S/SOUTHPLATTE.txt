SOUTHPLATTE SERIES

Depth class: Deep and very deep
Drainage class: Well drained
Saturated hydraulic conductivity: High
Parent material: Thin human-transported material over fine-loamy eolian deposits derived from glaciofluvial sources
Geomorphic location: Human-transported fill on plains mantled with mixed eolian deposits
Slope range: 0 to 3 percent
Mean annual temperature: 11.0 degrees C (51.8 degrees F)
Mean annual precipitation: 406 millimeters (16.0 inches)

Taxonomic Class:
Fine-loamy, mixed, superactive, mesic Aridic Haplustepts

Typical Pedon:
Southplatte loam in urban, park. (Colors are for dry soil unless otherwise noted.)

^A--0 to 18 cm; brown (10YR 4/3) loam, very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) moist; weak medium fine subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky, slightly plastic; noneffervescent, neutral (pH 7.2); clear wavy boundary.                                                   

2Bwb-18 to 33 cm; yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) sandy loam, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; weak coarse subangular blocky structure, soft, very friable, non-sticky, non-plastic; 2 percent fine gravel; noneffervescent; neutral  (pH 7.3); clear wavy boundary.      

^2Bkb--33 to 78 cm; brown (10YR 5/3) sandy loam, brown (10YR 4/3); weak coarse  subangular block structure; slightly hard, friable, non-sticky, non-plastic; finely disseminated free carbonates and 2 percent medium, irregular carbonates masses throughout the matrix; strongly effervescent; moderately alkaline (pH 8.2); gradual wavy boundary.   

2C1-78 to 107 cm; yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) sandy loam, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) moist; massive, slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky, slightly plastic; noneffervescent; neutral (pH 7.4); gradual way boundary.      

2C2--107 to 135 cm; yellowish brown (10YR 5/6) sandy clay loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4); massive; soft, very friable, non-sticky, non-plastic; few fine roots; noneffervescent; neutral (pH 7.2).

 2C3--135 to 200 cm; brownish yellow (10YR 6/6) fine sandy loam, light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) moist; massive; soft, very friable, non-sticky, non-plastic; noneffervescent; neutral (pH 6.8).

Competing Series: These are the Atchison, Berthoud, Fairden, Kachina, McRae, Redmountain, Sixmile series, Teromote, and Ziggy series.  None of these series has an ^A horizon.

Geographic Settings:
Parent material- Thin human-transported material over fine-loamy eolian deposits derived from glaciofluvial sources
Landform--Human-transported fill on plains mantled with mixed eolian deposits
Slope ranges--0 to 3 percent
Mean annual air temperature--10.0 to 12.0 degrees C (50.0 to 53.6 degrees F)
Mean annual precipitation range--356 to 432 millimeters (14.0 to 17.0 inches)
Frost-free period--135 to 165 days
Elevation--1,580 to 1,720 meters (~5,185 to 5,645 feet)
Climate--continental, temperate, semi-arid; very cold and snowy winters; cool, moist springs, hot summers, with North American monsoon precipitation from July through August.

Geographically Associated Soils: Globeville soils: Found on adjacent stream terraces and has fine-loamy particle size control section

Drainage and Saturated Hydraulic Conductivity:
Drainage class: Well drained
Saturated hydraulic conductivity: High (5 to 15 mm/sec)
Flooding: Southplatte soils are typically not subject to flooding
Ponding: Southplatte soils are typically not subject to ponding

Use and Vegetation:
Most areas are primarily used for urban development. The potential native vegetation is fourwing saltbush, winterfat, western wheatgrass, and blue grama.

Distribution and Extent: Found on plains, strath terraces, and pediments throughout the City and County of Denver, CO; small extent in north-central Colorado; MLRA 67B.

Soil Survey Region (SSR) Responsible:
Bozeman, Montana

Series Proposed:
Denver County, CO 2025. Southplatte series is the name of the river that flows through the City and County of Denver.
