REZANOF SERIES

Depth class: Very deep
Drainage class: Well drained
Parent material: herbaceous organic material over medial silty volcanic ash over gravelly loamy till
Landscape: Islands 
Landform: plains and hills 
Slopes: 0 to 20 percent
Mean annual precipitation: 600 to 2000 mm (23 to 78 in)
Mean annual temperature: 0 to 6 degrees C. (32 to 43 F.)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Medial over loamy-skeletal, amorphic over mixed, superactive Andic Haplocryolls

TYPICAL PEDON: Rezanof medial silt loam - on a grass herbaceous and shrubby plains at elevation of 80 meters (275 feet). (All colors are for moist soil.)

 Oe--0 to 8 centimeters (0 to 3 inches); very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2), brown (7.5YR 4/2), dry; moderately decomposed plant material; soft, very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; many very fine, fine, and common medium and coarse roots throughout; strongly acid (pH 5.3); clear smooth boundary. 

 A1--8 to 18 centimeters (3 to 7 inches); very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2), brown (7.5YR 4/2), dry; medial silt loam; weak fine granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; many fine, common medium, and common coarse roots throughout; moderately acid (pH 5.6); gradual smooth boundary. 

 A2--18 to 36 centimeters (7 to 14.2nches); very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2), brown (7.5YR 4/2), dry; medial silt loam; weak fine subangular blocky structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; common (3.0) medium roots throughout and common (5.0) fine roots; fragments; moderately acid, pH 5.7; gradual wavy boundary. 

 Bw--36 to 94 centimeters (14 to 37 inches); medial silt loam; 50 percent sand; 6 percent clay; moderate medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; many fine, common medium, and common coarse roots throughout; 5 percent gravel; moderately acid (pH 5.6); abrupt wavy boundary. 

 2C--94 to 200 centimeters (37 to 79 inches); dark grayish brown (2.5Y 4/2) very gravelly sandy loam; massive; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky, nonplastic; 50 percent gravel and 5 percent cobble; very strongly acid (pH 5.0). 

TYPE LOCATION: Kodiak Archipelago Soil Survey Area, Alaska; Latitude 57 degrees 23 minutes 38.99 seconds and Longitude 154 degrees 24 minutes 34.00 seconds west, UTM north 6362141.94 and UTM east 415290.08, zone 5, Datum WGS84.

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These include Akalura, Aleneva, Alitak, Koniag, Luchek, Sulua, Terror Lake, Tonki,  Tungalara, and Wineskey series.
Akalura soils occur on mountain, hill, or ridge on flanks, backslopes, footslopes and toeslopes.
Aleneva soils have aquic features at depths greater between 41 to 100 cm (16 to 40 in) and occur on drainageways and depressions.
Alitak soils have thick organic material and occur in drainageways and depressions.
Koniag soils lack mollic epipedons and occur on similar landform positions.
Luchek soils have thick organic material over skeletal glacial till and occur in drainageways and depressions.
Narrow Cape soils have recent volcanic ash surface horizons and occur on similar landform positions.
Sulua soils have thick organic material over skeletal glacial till and occur on drainageways and depressions.
Terror Lake soils have aquic features at depths greater between 41 to 100 cm (16 to 40 in) and occur on drainageways and depressions.
Tonki soils have aquic features within 0 to 25 cm (0 to 10 in) and occur on drainageways and depressions.
Tungalara soils have skeletal particle-sized control sections and occur on similar positions.
Wineskey soils have aquic features at depths greater between 41 to 100 cm (16 to 40 in) and occur on drainageways and depressions.

DRAINAGE AND PERMEABILITY: 
Drainage class: well drained
Saturated Hydraulic Conductivity: very high in the Oe horizon, moderately high or high in the A and Bw horizons, and high or very high in the 2C.
Permeability: rapid or very rapid in the Oe horizon, moderate to rapid in the A and Bw, and moderate to very rapid in the 2C horizons.
Runoff: low to medium.

USE AND VEGETATION: 
Used for water shed. Native vegetation is Sitka Spruce, Forb meadow and Grass.
