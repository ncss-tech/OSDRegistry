PEGATI SERIES

Landscape--mountains, drift plains
Landform--swales and valley sides
Slope--2 to 40 percent
Parent material--colluvium with volcanic ash influence in the upper part
Mean annual precipitation--about 1200 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 0 degrees C
Depth class--very deep
Drainage class--somewhat poorly drained
Soil moisture regime--aquic
Soil temperature regime--cryic
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Medial over loamy, amorphic over isotic, nonacid Typic Cryaquands

TYPICAL PEDON: Pegati medial sandy loam on a north-northeast-facing, linear and concave swale of a mountain, on a slope of 17 percent at an elevation of 156 m (When described on August 7, 2013, the soil was moist to a depth of 61 cm and saturated throughout the rest of the profile.)

 Oi--0 to 2 cm; slightly decomposed plant material; many very fine and fine and common medium to very coarse roots; very strongly acid (pH 4.7); clear irregular boundary

 A--2 to 13 cm; medial sandy loam, dark reddish gray (5YR 4/2) dry, dark reddish brown (5YR 2.5/2) moist; weak fine granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; many very fine and common fine to very coarse roots; strongly acid (pH 5.1); clear irregular boundary

 AB1--13 to 26 cm; medial sandy loam, matrix color 60 percent brown (7.5YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (7.5YR 3/3) moist, 20 percent pale brown (10YR 6/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist, and 20 percent grayish brown (10YR 5/2) dry, very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) moist; weak medium subangular blocky structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine and fine roots; moderately acid (pH 5.7); clear smooth boundary

 AB2--26 to 38 cm; medial sandy loam, brown (7.5YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (7.5YR 3/3) moist; weak medium granular structure; slightly hard, friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine and fine roots; 10 percent very coarse iron depletions, dark grayish brown (10YR 4/2) moist, clear boundaries, and 20 percent very coarse masses of oxidized iron, dark reddish brown (5YR 3/4) moist, clear boundaries; 1 percent gravel; moderately acid (pH 6.0); clear smooth boundary

 Bg--38 to 60 cm; gravelly medial loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) moist; moderate fine granular structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and nonplastic; common very fine and fine roots; 35 percent iron depletions, brown (10YR 4/3) moist, diffuse boundaries, and 5 percent medium masses of oxidized iron, brown (7.5YR 4/4) moist, diffuse boundaries; 18 percent gravel; slightly acid (pH 6.1); clear smooth boundary

 2Cg--60 to 150 cm; gravelly sandy loam, light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) moist; massive; soft, very friable, moderately sticky and nonplastic; 30 percent very coarse iron depletions, olive brown (2.5Y 4/3) moist, clear boundaries, and 3 percent medium masses of oxidized iron, strong brown (7.5YR 4/6) moist, clear boundaries; 25 percent gravel; moderately acid (pH 5.9)

TYPE LOCATION: Dillingham Census Area, Alaska, about 50 km west-northwest of the city of Dillingham; latitude 59.2353398, longitude -159.2148575, datum WGS 84 (coordinates determined with a GPS)

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--somewhat poorly drained
Saturation in normal years--below a depth of 0 to 25 cm in April through June, below a depth of 25 to 65 cm throughout the rest of the year
Saturated hydraulic conductivity (Ksat)--moderately high to very high in the A, AB, and Bg horizons, moderately low to high in the 2Cg horizon
