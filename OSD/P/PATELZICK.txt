PATELZICK SERIES

Landscape--alluvial plains
Landform--fan remnants
Slope--1 to 12 percent
Parent material--loess-influenced alluvium over rhyolite
Mean annual precipitation--about 350 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 4 degrees C
Depth class--shallow
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--xeric
Soil temperature regime--frigid

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, mixed, superactive, frigid, shallow Typic Durixerolls

TYPICAL PEDON: Patelzick stony loam on a 10-percent slope in an area of rangeland at an elevation of 1778 m

 A--0 to 8 cm; stony loam, grayish brown (10YR 5/2) dry, very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) moist; weak thin platy structure; slightly hard, very friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; common very fine roots; common very fine irregular pores; 10 percent gravel, 3 percent flagstones, and 3 percent stones; slightly alkaline (pH 7.4); clear smooth boundary

 Bt--8 to 20 cm; very gravelly clay loam, grayish brown (10YR 5/2) dry, very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, very friable, slightly sticky and moderately plastic; many very fine and fine roots; common very fine tubular pores; few thin clay films on ped faces; 25 percent gravel and 10 percent flagstones; slightly alkaline (pH 7.4); clear wavy boundary

 Bk1--20 to 36 cm; very gravelly loam, pale brown (10YR 6/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; moderate fine subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, very friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; common very fine and fine roots; few very fine tubular pores; 30 percent gravel, 15 percent flagstones, and 3 percent stones; strongly effervescent; slightly alkaline (pH 7.6); clear irregular boundary

 Bk2--36 to 43 cm; very gravelly sandy loam, very pale brown (10YR 7/3) dry, brown (10YR 5/3) moist; weak very fine subangular blocky structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine roots; few very fine tubular pores; 40 percent gravel, 5 percent flagstones, and 2 percent stones; carbonate coatings on bottom of rock fragments; violently effervescent; moderately alkaline (pH 8.2); abrupt wavy boundary

 Bkqm--43 to 48 cm; moderately cemented duripan, very pale brown (10YR 8/2) dry, pale brown (10YR 6/3) moist; abrupt smooth boundary

 R--48 cm; rhyolite

TYPE LOCATION: Clark County, Idaho; about 8.9 km southwest of Spencer, Idaho; about 553 m north and 356 m east of the southwest corner of section 36, T. 12 N., R. 35 E.; Thunder Gulch U.S. Geological Survey quadrangle; latitude 44.32489 degrees north, longitude 112.28835 degrees west, datum WGS 84 (coordinates determined with a GPS)
