Landscape--mountains

Microfeature--earth hummock

Mean annual precipitation--about 570 mm

Depth class--very deep

Soil moisture regime--aquic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-loamy, isotic, acid Aeric Cryaquepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Peke silt loam on a west southwest facing linear, linear footslope on a slope of 14 percent at an elevation of 481 m (The soil was moist from 0 to 150 cm when described on July 15, 2019.)

 Oi--0 to 2 cm; slightly decomposed plant material, dark grayish brown (10YR 4/2) dry, very dark brown (10YR 2/2) moist; common very fine, fine, medium, coarse, and very coarse roots; strongly acid (pH 5.3); clear smooth boundary

 A--2 to 7 cm; silt loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak fine granular structure; very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; weakly smeary; common very fine, fine, medium, coarse, and very coarse roots; very strongly acid (pH 5.0); clear smooth boundary

 Bwjj--7 to 38 cm; silt loam, 60 percent yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) and 40 percent brown (10YR 5/3) dry, 60 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) and 40 percent dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; moderate coarse subangular blocky structure; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; common very fine and fine roots; common fine irregular pores; 1 percent subangular gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.1); clear wavy boundary

 2Bw--38 to 67 cm; gravelly silt loam, light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) broken face dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) broken face moist; weak thin platy structure; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; common very fine and fine roots; common very fine irregular and common fine irregular pores; 10 percent medium strong brown (7.5YR 4/6), moist, masses of oxidized iron and 20 percent coarse brown (10YR 5/3), moist, iron depletions; 15 percent subangular gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.5); clear wavy boundary

 3C--67 to 150 cm; very gravelly loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) dry, dark yellowish brown  (10YR 3/4) moist; massive; firm, slightly sticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; common very fine roots; common very fine irregular pores; 5 percent subangular cobbles, 45 percent subangular gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.3)
