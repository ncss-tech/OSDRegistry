PELICAN REEF SERIES

MLRA(s)--152A
Depth class--Very deep
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Water depth range--0 to 2 meters (0 to 6 feet)
Parent material--Sandy lagoonal deposits
Geomorphic location--Relict flood-tidal deltas
Slope range--0 to 1 percent
Mean annual air temperature--21 degrees C (70 degrees F)
Mean annual water temperature--24 degrees C (75 degrees F)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Siliceous, hyperthermic Fluventic Psammowassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Pelican Reef loamy sand on a relict flood-tidal delta under 0.7 meters of salt water. (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 Aseg--0 to 9 centimeters; black (N 2.5/) loamy sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; strong sulfurous odor; many fine roots throughout; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (3.5 dS/m); slightly alkaline (pH 7.7), neutral (pH 7.3) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg1--9 to 48 centimeters; 95 percent dark grayish olive (10Y 4/2) and 5 percent black (N 2.5/) loamy sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; common fine roots throughout; 8 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (3.2 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 7.9), strongly alkaline (pH 8.9) oxidized pH; clear boundary

 Cseg2--48 to 153 centimeters; 92 percent light grayish olive (10Y 6/2) and 8 percent dark grayish olive (10Y 4/2) sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 10 percent shell fragments at the top of the horizon; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (3.6 dS/m); slightly alkaline (pH 7.8), very strongly alkaline (pH 9.6) oxidized pH; gradual boundary

 Cseg3--153 to 200 centimeters; 95 percent light greenish gray (10Y 7/1) and 5 percent dark grayish olive (10Y 4/2) sand; single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 5 percent shell fragments; no sulfurous odor; no color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; saline (3.5 dS/m); slightly alkaline (pH 7.7), very strongly alkaline (pH 9.3) oxidized pH

TYPE LOCATION: Franklin County, Florida; Saint George Sound; approximately 0.5 kilometers (0.3 miles) north of the southeast point of Goose Island, approximately 2.5 kilometers (1.6 miles) northeast by north of Shell Point, and approximately 1.6 kilometers (1.0 miles) west by northwest of Gap Point; USGS topographic quadrangle: Goose Island, FL

COMPETING SERIES:
These are the Broadaxe, Eastpoint, Lutz, McKissack, Mullet, Sandbank, and Thalinectes series.
Broadaxe soils--Formed on submerged wave-cut platforms and contain buried spodic horizons.
Eastpoint soils--Formed on submerged wave-cut platforms, have slightly lower water salinity, and can have oxidized reactions below 7.4.
Lutz soils--Formed on flood-tidal deltas and have lower organic carbon densities in the surface.
McKissack soils--Formed in estuarine tidal streams from sandy estuarine deposits and commonly have 7.5YR hue and color values of 3 or 4. 
Mullet soils--Formed on washover-fan flats, can have greater shell percentages and have lower organic carbon densities in the surface.
Sandbank soils--Formed on estuarine tidal stream deltas and have very fine mica flakes throughout the profile.
Thalinectes soils--Formed on washover-fans and longshore bars, allow for redder hue and can have oxidized reactions below 7.4

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Landscape--Bays and Lagoons along the Gulf Coast
Landform--Relict (inactive) flood-tidal deltas
Parent material--Sandy lagoonal deposits 
Slope--0 to 1 percent
Water depth range--0 to 2 meters (0 to 6 feet) below mean sea level
Water salinity range--25 to 35 parts per thousand
Water temperature range--18 to 30 degrees C (64 to 86 degrees F)
Soil temperature range--19 to 27 degrees C (66 to 81 degrees F)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Amtrac, Apalachicola, Dry Bar, and Sheepshead soils.
Amtrac soils--Are on high-energy lagoon bottoms and barrier coves and formed in coarse-loamy lagoon deposits.
Apalachicola soils--Are on bay and lagoon bottoms and formed in fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Dry Bar soils--Are on shoals and reefs and formed in sandy-skeletal lagoonal deposits. 
Sheepshead soils--Are in barrier coves and do not have sandy textures throughout the particle size control section. 

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--High to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish water.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--These soils are used for recreational fishing and boating. These soils are occasionally dredged. 
Typical vegetation--Often consists of shoal grass (Halodule wrightii) or turtle grass (Thalassia testudinum)
