POKERCREEK SERIES

Landscape--hills, valleys
Landform--hillslopes
Slope--1 to 10 percent
Parent material--mossy organic material over loess over loamy colluvium
Mean annual precipitation--about 490 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about -3 degrees C
Depth class--very deep
Drainage class--poorly drained
Soil moisture regime--aquic
Soil temperature regime--gelic
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-loamy, isotic, acid, subgelic Typic Gelaquepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Pokercreek silt loam on a south southeast facing linear, linear toeslope of a hill on a slope of 4 percent at an elevation of 132 m (The soil was moist throughout the soil profile when described on June 20, 2016.)

 Oi--0 to 13 cm; slightly decomposed plant material, reddish yellow (7.5YR 6/6) dry, strong brown (7.5YR 4/6) moist; nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine, fine, and very coarse roots throughout, and many medium and coarse roots throughout; extremely acid (pH 3.6); clear smooth boundary

 Oa--13 to 22 cm; highly decomposed plant material, grayish brown (10YR 5/2) dry very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) moist; nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine, fine, and very coarse roots throughout and many medium and coarse roots throughout; extremely acid (pH 4.0); abrupt wavy boundary

 C1--22 to 64 cm; silt loam, pale yellow (2.5Y 7/3) dry, light olive brown (2.5Y 5/3) moist; massive; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine, fine, and medium roots throughout; few fine tubular pores; 20 percent coarse distinct irregular noncemented dark gray (10YR 4/1) moist, masses of reduced iron with clear boundaries in matrix and 25 percent medium prominent irregular noncemented strong brown (7.5YR 5/6) moist, masses of oxidized iron with clear boundaries in matrix; strongly acid (pH 5.2); gradual smooth boundary

 C2--64 to 150 cm; silt loam, pale yellow (2.5Y 7/3) dry, light olive brown (2.5Y 5/3) moist; massive; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; 35 percent medium distinct irregular noncemented dark gray (2.5Y 4/1), moist, masses of reduced iron with clear boundaries in matrix; 3 percent subrounded gravel, 10 percent subrounded fine gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.3)

TYPE LOCATION: Yukon-Koyukuk Census Area, Alaska, latitude 63.0529800 longitude -160.8009900 degrees, datum WGS84 (coordinates determined with a GPS unit)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
*Cilla--ochric epipedon, depth to aquic conditions below 50 cm, cambic horizon present, gravel content ranges above 15 percent; toeslopes, footslopes, and northfacing backslopes of hills
*Imak--aquic conditions at the soil surface, cambic horizon present, permafrost present; similar landscape position
*Jacksoncreek--histic epipedon, reduced matrix, cryoturbation present, texture ranges to very fine sandy loam; stream terraces in valleys

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:

*Saturation during normal years--saturated at a depth of between 0 and 25 cm for at least two weeks during the growing season
*Saturated hydraulic conductivity (Ksat)--very high in the folistic epipedon, moderately high throughout the rest of the profile
