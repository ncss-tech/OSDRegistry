TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, mixed, superactive Typic Humicryepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Tolstoipoint sandy loam on an east facing linear, linear backslope, on a slope of 45 percent at an elevation of 515 m (The soil was moist throughout when described on June 20, 2016.)

 Oa--0 to 4 cm; highly decomposed plant material ,brown (7.5YR) dry, dark brown (7.5YR 3/4) moist; nonsticky, nonplastic; many very fine and common fine, medium, and coarse roots throughout; very strongly acid (pH 4.5); abrupt smooth boundary

 A--4 to 27 cm; very cobbly sandy loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure; friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine, fine, medium, and coarse roots throughout; common very fine and fine irregular pores; 5 percent angular flagstones and 10 percent angular channers and 10 percent subangular fine gravel and 10 percent subangular gravel and 20 percent subangular cobbles; very strongly acid (pH 5.0); clear wavy boundary

 CA--27 to 60 cm; extremely cobbly sandy loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) moist; massive; very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine, medium, and coarse and many fine roots throughout; common very fine and fine irregular pores; 10 percent subangular fine gravel and 15 percent angular flagstones and 15 percent angular channers and 18 percent subangular gravel and 20 percent subangular cobbles; strongly acid (pH 5.4); gradual wavy boundary

 C--60 to 150 cm; extremely cobbly sandy loam, light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) broken face moist; single grain; loose, nonsticky, nonplastic; common fine roots; 8 percent subangular fine gravel and 12 percent subangular gravel and 15 percent angular channers and 20 percent angular flagstones and 25 percent subangular cobbles; moderately acid (pH 5.7)
