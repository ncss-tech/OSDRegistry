FORTYDAY SERIES

Landscape--plateaus
Landform--south-facing slopes of structural benches and ridges
Slope--0 to 70 percent
Parent material--residuum and colluvium derived from basalt and a small amount of loess
Mean annual precipitation--about 200 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 10 degrees C
Depth class--shallow
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--aridic
Soil temperature regime--mesic
Soil moisture subclass--xeric

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, mixed, superactive, mesic Lithic Xeric Haplargids

TYPICAL PEDON: Fortyday very gravelly silt loam, 25 percent slopes, in an area of rangeland

 A--0 to 10 cm; very gravelly silt loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak medium granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and slightly plastic; common very fine and fine and few medium roots; common fine tubular pores; 35 percent gravel and 15 percent surface cobbles; moderately alkaline (pH 8.0); clear wavy boundary

 AB--10 to 20 cm; gravelly silt loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak coarse prismatic structure; slightly hard, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine and fine and few medium roots; few fine tubular pores; 20 percent gravel; moderately alkaline (pH 8.0); clear wavy boundary

 Bt1--20 to 28 cm; very cobbly sandy loam, light yellowish brown (10YR 6/4) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; few very fine and fine roots; few fine tubular pores; few thin colloids and bridges between mineral grains; 40 percent cobbles and 10 percent gravel; moderately alkaline (pH 8.l); clear wavy boundary

 Bt2--28 to 48 cm; extremely cobbly loam, brown (7.5YR 4/4) dry, brown (7.5YR 4/3) moist; moderate fine subangular blocky structure; hard, friable, slightly sticky and plastic; few very fine roots; few fine tubular pores; common thin clay films on faces of peds; 55 percent cobbles and 10 percent gravel; moderately alkaline (pH 8.0); abrupt irregular boundary

 R--48 cm; basalt

TYPE LOCATION: Yakama Nation, Yakima County, Washington, about 11 km southwest of Toppenish; about 400 m east and 240 m north of the southwest corner of section 3, T. 9 N., R. 19 E.; latitude 46.288577, longitude -120.434409, datum WGS 84 (coordinates estimated from PLSS details)

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Elevation--150 to 670 m
Climate--warm, dry summers; cool, moist winters
Mean annual precipitation--150 to 230 mm
Mean annual air temperature--9 to 11 degrees C
Frost-free season--130 to 195 days

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--well drained

USE AND VEGETATION:
Use--livestock grazing
Vegetation--dominantly purple sage, Sandberg bluegrass, bluebunch wheatgrass, and eriogonum

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: South-central Washington in Yakima County; MLRAs 7, 8; small extent

SOIL SURVEY REGIONAL OFFICE (SSRO) RESPONSIBLE: Portland, Oregon
