TYPICAL PEDON: False Cape loamy sand on a submerged wave-built terrace under 1.5 meters of brackish water (colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated; electrical conductivity is 1:5 by volume solution mixture unless otherwise stated).

 Cseg1--0 to 28 centimeters; olive gray (5Y 5/2) loamy sand; structureless, single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 14 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.6 dS/m); neutral (pH 7.1); clear boundary.

 Cseg2--28 to 71 centimeters; olive gray (5Y 4/2) sand; structureless, single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; no effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.5 dS/m); slightly alkaline (pH 7.5); abrupt boundary.

 2Cseg3--71 to 120 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (10Y 3/1) clay loam; structureless, massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; moderately fluid; 3 percent shell fragments; moderate sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.4 dS/m); slightly alkaline (pH 7.8); clear boundary.

 2Cseg4--120 to 146 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (10Y 3/1) shelly loam; structureless, massive; slightly sticky, slightly plastic; moderately fluid; 15 percent shell fragments; moderate sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.5 dS/m); slightly alkaline (pH 7.7); clear boundary.

 3Cseg5--146 to 200 centimeters; dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1) loamy sand; structureless, single grain; nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; 2 percent shell fragments; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide; slight effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; nonsaline (0.5 dS/m); moderately alkaline (pH 7.9).

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Kill Devil, Lost, Trinity Harbor and Whalehead soils.
Kill Devil soils--are on low-energy lagoon bottoms, fluviomarine bottoms, and mainland coves and formed in fine-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Lost soils--are on submerged tidal marshes, have O horizons, and contain hypersulfidic materials.
Trinity Harbor soils--are on high-energy lagoon bottoms and formed in coarse-loamy lagoonal deposits.
Whalehead soils--are on submerged wave-cut platforms and formed in sandy lagoonal deposits over sandy fluviomarine deposits.

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately low to high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic
Soil is continuously inundated by brackish water. The water level is primarily driven by wind tides.

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major uses--The area is used for recreational fishing, swimming, hunting, and boating. Uses also include commercial fishing and crabbing.
Typical vegetation--No vegetation was observed during ecological inventories of False Cape soils, perhaps due to investigations taking place too early in the growing season. However, that does not rule out the possibility of these soils supporting SAV. Vegetation may include widgeon grass (Ruppia maritima), sago pondweed (Stuckenia pectinata), and Eurasian milfoil (Myriophyllum spicatum).

REMARKS:
The series is named after an area on Virginia's Atlantic coast that deceptively resembled Cape Henry. This led to numerous shallow-water shipwrecks during the 19th century and gave False Cape the reputation as a shipping graveyard. False Cape soils were previously mapped as water in North Carolina and Virginia soil surveys.

Diagnostic horizons and other diagnostic soil characteristics recognized in this pedon are (Keys to Soil Taxonomy, Thirteenth Edition, 2022):
Fluidity--The zone from 71 to 146 centimeters has a fluidity class of moderately fluid.
Lithologic discontinuity--The zone from 71 to 146 centimeters and 146 to 200 centimeters formed in different depositional energies.
Peraquic feature--Zero or positive soil water potential at the soil surface (permanently submerged).
