GULER SERIES

Landscape--mountain valleys
Landform--valley sides
Slope--0 to 20 percent
Parent material--volcanic ash and colluvium derived from basalt
Mean annual precipitation--about 890 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 8 degrees C
Depth class--very deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--xeric
Soil temperature regime--mesic
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Ashy-skeletal, amorphic, mesic Humic Vitrixerands

TYPICAL PEDON: Guler ashy sandy loam under a ponderosa pine forest, on a 9-percent, southwest-facing slope at an elevation of 575 m (All textures are apparent field textures.)

 Oi--0 to 3 cm; undecomposed needles, leaves, and twigs

 A1--3 to 15 cm; ashy sandy loam, very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) dry, very dark brown (10YR 2/2) moist; weak very fine and fine granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; weakly smeary; many very fine and fine and few medium roots; many very fine and fine irregular pores; 10 percent gravel; NaF pH 10.5; moderately acid (pH 5.8); clear wavy boundary

 A2--15 to 30 cm; gravelly ashy sandy loam, brown (10YR 4/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak very fine and fine granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; weakly smeary; many very fine and fine and few medium roots; many very fine and fine irregular pores; 15 percent gravel; NaF pH 10.5, slightly acid (pH 6.5); clear smooth boundary

 Bw--30 to 66 cm; gravelly ashy fine sandy loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/4) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) moist; weak fine and very fine subangular blocky structure; soft, friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; very fine and fine irregular pores; 15 percent gravel and 5 percent cobbles; NaF pH 10.5, slightly acid (pH 6.5); clear wavy boundary

 BC--66 to 155 cm; extremely gravelly ashy fine sandy loam, yellowish brown (10YR 5/6) dry, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/6) moist; weak fine and very fine subangular blocky structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic; common very fine and few medium roots; many very fine and fine irregular pores; 50 percent gravel, 10 percent cobbles, and 10 percent stones; slightly acid (pH 6.2)

TYPE LOCATION: Klickitat County, Washington, about 5 km southwest of Glenwood; about 300 m north and 610 m west of the southeast corner of section 19, T. 6 N., R. 12 E.; latitude 45.989144, longitude -121.352729, datum WGS 84 (coordinates estimated from section, township, and range)
