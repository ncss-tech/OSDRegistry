YAMASH SERIES

Landscape--mountains, plateaus, canyonlands
Landform--mountain slopes, structural benches, canyons
Slope--0 to 60 percent
Parent material--volcanic ash over colluvium and residuum derived from basalt
Mean annual precipitation--about 760 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 6 degrees C
Depth class--moderately deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--xeric
Soil temperature regime--frigid
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, isotic, frigid Andic Haploxeralfs

TYPICAL PEDON: Yamash ashy fine sandy loam in an area of forestland, on a 3-percent, east-facing slope at an elevation of 1500 m (All textures are apparent field textures.)

 Oi--0 to 5 cm; slightly decomposed forest litter

 A--5 to 15 cm; ashy fine sandy loam, very dark grayish brown (10YR 3/2) dry, very dark brown (10YR 2/2) moist; weak fine granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky and nonplastic, weakly smeary; common fine and medium roots; few fine irregular pores; moderately acid (pH 6.0), NaF pH 10.0; abrupt wavy boundary

 Bw--15 to 33 cm; ashy fine sandy loam, brown (7.5YR 4/4) dry, dark brown (7.5YR 3/4) moist; weak fine subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and nonplastic; weakly smeary; many medium and coarse and common fine roots; common fine irregular pores; 10 percent weathered basalt paragravel; moderately acid (pH 6.0), NaF pH 9.8; gradual wavy boundary

 2Bt1--33 to 48 cm; very cobbly loam, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) dry, dark brown (7.5YR 3/4) moist; weak medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, firm, moderately sticky and moderately plastic; common medium and coarse and few fine roots; common very fine and few fine tubular pores; common distinct clay films on faces of peds and in pores; 15 percent gravel, 20 percent cobbles, and 1 percent stones; 10 percent weathered basalt paragravel and 15 percent paracobbles; moderately acid (pH 6.0), NaF pH 9.6; clear irregular boundary

 2Bt2--48 to 66 cm; very cobbly loam, dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/4) dry, dark brown (7.5YR 3/4) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, firm, moderately sticky and moderately plastic; few fine and medium roots; common fine and few medium tubular pores; many distinct clay films on faces of peds and in pores; 10 percent gravel, 25 percent cobbles, and 10 percent stones; 10 percent weathered basalt paragravel and 20 percent paracobbles; slightly acid (pH 6.5), NaF pH 9.6; abrupt irregular boundary

 2Cr--66 cm; highly weathered basalt

TYPE LOCATION: Yakama Nation, Yakima County, Washington, about 400 m south of Olney Corral; about 430 m west and 110 m south of the northeast corner of section 21, T. 10 N., R. 14 E., Willamette Meridian; latitude 46.3467642, longitude -121.0683820, datum WGS 84 (coordinates estimated from section, township, and range)

RANGE IN CHARACTERISTICS:
Mean annual soil temperature at a depth of 50 cm--6 to 8 degrees C
Moisture control section--usually moist, but dry in all parts 45 to 60 consecutive days following summer solstice
Depth to paralithic contact--50 to 100 cm
Particle-size control section (argillic horizon)--35 to 60 percent hard rock fragments; 15 to 60 percent soft basalt pararock fragments; 18 to 35 percent clay

Volcanic ash mantle (A and Bw horizons)
*Thickness--20 to 35 cm
*Moist bulk density--0.75 to 0.9 g/cm3
*Glass content--5 to 30 percent
*Ammonium oxalate aluminum plus one-half iron--2 to 3 percent
*15-bar water retention--12 to 15 percent (air-dried samples)

Oi horizon
Thickness--0 to 8 cm

Thickness--5 to 10 cm

Chroma--3 or 4 dry or moist
Texture--ashy fine sandy loam, gravelly ashy fine sandy loam, ashy sandy loam
Thickness--13 to 23 cm

2Bt horizon
Hue--7.5YR, 10YR

Chroma--4 to 6 dry or moist

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: East slopes of Cascade Mountains in Washington, Yakima County, Yakama Nation; MLRA 6; small extent

Yamash is a Yakama word for deer. White Deer Creek is near the type location for this series. In 2004, the series originally was named the Whitedeer series.
