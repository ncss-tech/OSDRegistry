INGIK SERIES

Depth class: moderately deep to paralithic bedrock
Drainage class: well drained
Parent material: gravelly loamy colluvium over residuum weathered from mudstone
Landscape: mountains
Landform: mountain slopes
Slopes: 10 to 80 percent
Mean annual precipitation: about 500 mm (20 in)
Mean annual air temperature: about -4 degrees C. (25 degrees F.)
Frost-free period: 70 to 85 days

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, mixed, superactive, nonacid Typic Cryorthents

TYPICAL PEDON: Ingik silt loam in a dryas lichen alpine community on a southeast facing convex, linear backslope on a slope of 30 percent at an elevation of 740m (2428 ft)

     Oe--0 to 9 cm (0 to 3.5 in); moderately decomposed plant material; very dark brown (10YR 2/2) moist, dark grayish brown (10YR 4/2) dry; many very fine and fine roots throughout; strongly acid (pH 5.1); abrupt smooth boundary. (3 to 12 cm, 1 to 5 in thick)

     A--9 to 10 cm (3.5 to 4 in); silt loam; black (10YR 2/1) moist, dark gray (10YR 4/1) dry; weak fine granular structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; many very fine and fine roots throughout; common very fine and fine irregular pores; 5 percent gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.2); abrupt smooth boundary. (0 to 15 cm, 0 to 6 in thick)

     C--10 to 55 cm (4 to 22 in); extremely cobbly silt loam; dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist, brown (10YR 5/3) dry; massive; very friable, soft, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; common very fine, many fine, and few medium  roots throughout; common very fine and fine irregular pores; 35 percent gravel and 30 percent cobbles; moderately acid (pH 5.7); clear wavy boundary. (42 to 50 cm, 17 to 20 in thick)

     2Cr--55 to 80 cm (22 to 32 in); moderately coherent mudstone bedrock.

TYPE LOCATION: Nulato Hills - Continental Divide and Blackburn Hills Soil Survey Area, Alaska; latitude 64.5426300, longitude -159.9088900, datum WGS84, UTM north 7157795.37 and UTM east 456409.50, zone 4, datum WGS84.

COMPETING SERIES: These are the Dagelet, Enigma (AK), Kaina (MT), Bracos (NM), Gralic, and Jackmore (NV) series.
Dagelet soils have a lithic contact at depths between 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in), and have a mean annual air temperature range of 0 to 9 degrees C. (32 to 48 degrees F.)
Engima soils have a lithic contact at depths between 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in), have free carbonates at the surface, and have a mean annual air temperature range of 1 to 9 degrees C. (34 to 48 degrees F.)
Kaina soils lack a paralithic contact within 200 cm (79 in), and have a mean annual air temperature range of 0 to 4 degrees C. (32 to 39 degrees F.)
Bracos soils have a lithic contact at depths between 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in), and have a mean annual air temperature range of 2 to 4.5 degrees C. (36 to 40 degrees F.)
Gralic soils lack a paralithic contact within 200 cm (79 in), and have a mean annual air temperature range of 2 to 4 degrees C. (36 to 39 degrees F.)
Jackmore soils lack a paralithic contact within 200 cm (79 in), have a xeric soile moisture regime, and have a mean annual air temperature range of 3 to 6 degrees C. (37 to 43 degrees F.)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These are the Aklak, Apkun, Atkaktuk, Continentaldivide, Debauch, Iyyagrik, Kanik, Kulik, Managaarak, Nautchiak, and Piksrun soils.
Aklak soils have a lithic contact at depths between 100 to 150 cm (40 to 60 in), have a fragmental layer starting within 22 to 78 cm (9 to 31 in), have a mollic epipedon, have a fragmental particle size class, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on mountainflanks, upper and center thirds
Apkun soils have a lithic contact at depths between 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in), have a fragmental layer starting at depths between 35 to 84 cm (14 to 33 in), have an umbric epipedon, have an acid reaction class, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on mountainflanks, upper and center thirds
Atkaktuk soils lack a paralithic contact within 200 cm (79 in), have a fragmental layer starting at depths between 35 to 60 cm (14 to 24 in), have an umbric epipedon, have a loamy-skeletal over fragmental particle size class, have an acid reaction class, and a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on similar landforms on mountainflanks, upper third
Continentaldivide soils have a lithic contact at depths between 20 to 50 cm (8 to 20 in), have a mollic epipedon, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on ridges
Debauch soils have a paralithic contact at depths between 20 to 50 cm (8 to 20 in), have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on ridges
Iyyagrik soils have a lithic contact at depths between 100 to 150 cm (40 to 60 in), have a mollic epipedon, and occur on similar landforms on mountainflanks, upper third
Kanik soils have a lithic contact at depths between 100 to 150 cm (40 to 60 in), have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on mountainflanks, upper and center thirds
Kulik soils have a lithic contact at depths between 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in), have a mollic epipedon, have a cambic horizon, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on mountainflanks, upper and center thirds
Managaarak soils have a lithic contact at depths between 100 to 150 cm (40 to 60 in), have a fragmental layer starting at depths between 41 to 93 cm (16 to 37 in), have an acid reaction class, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on similar landforms on mountainflanks, upper third
Nautchiak soils lack a paralithic contact within 200 cm (79 in), have a cambic horizon, have an isotic mineralogy class, and occur on drainageways and swales
Piksrun soils lack a paralithic contact within 200 cm (79 in), have an umbric epipedon, have an acid reaction class, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on mountainflanks, upper and center thirds

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY: 
Drainage class: well drained
Saturated Hydraulic Conductivity: very high in the Oe horizon, and moderately high in the A and C horizons
Permeability: rapid to very rapid in the Oe horizon, and moderate in the A and C horizons
Runoff: high

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: MLRA 240, Nulato Hills-Southern Seward Peninsula Highlands. The series is of moderate extent.
