IVGICH SERIES

Depth class: very deep
Drainage class: very poorly drained
Parent material: loamy alluvium derived from metamorphic and sedimentary rock over loamy estuarine deposits
Landscape: coastal plains
Landform: depressions on delta plains, abandoned channels on delta plains
Slopes: 0 to 1 percent
Mean annual precipitation: about 350 mm (14 in)
Mean annual air temperature: about -3 degrees C. (27 degrees F.)
Frost-free period: 75 to 90 days

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Fine-loamy, mixed, superactive, nonacid Typic Sulfaquents

TYPICAL PEDON: Ivgich silt loam in a halophytic sedge estuary community on a concave, concave abandoned channel on a slope of 0 percent at an elevation of 3m (10 ft)

     Ag--0 to 3 cm (0 to 1 in); silt loam black (10YR 2/1) broken face moist, dark gray (10YR 4/1) broken face dry; weak fine granular structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; many very fine roots and common fine roots throughout; common fine dendritic tubular pores; no odor; positive to alpha-alpha dipyridyl measured in matrix; noneffervescent; neutral (pH 6.7); clear smooth boundary. (1 to 15 cm, 0.5 to 6 in thick)

     2Cseg1--3 to 19 cm (1 to 8 in); silt loam; black (N 2.5/), broken face moist, dark gray (N 4/), broken face dry; massive; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonfluid; many very fine roots and common fine roots throughout; common fine dendritic tubular pores; 0 mS of EC; slight sulfurous odor; reduced iron monosulfides present; positive to alpha-alpha dipyridyl measured in matrix; noneffervescent; slightly acid (pH 6.5), extremely acid after 16 weeks (oxidized pH 3.75); clear smooth boundary.      2Cseg2--19 to 60 cm (8 to 24 in); silt loam; greenish black (10Y 2.5/1) broken face moist, dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1) broken face dry; massive; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; slightly fluid; few very fine roots throughout; common very fine irregular pores; 2.4 mS of EC; slight sulfurous odor; reduced iron monosulfides present; positive to alpha-alpha dipyridyl measured in matrix; noneffervescent; slightly acid (pH 6.3),strongly acid after 16 weeks (oxidized pH 5.5); gradual smooth boundary. (30 to 70 cm, 12 to 28 in thick)

     2Cseg3--60 to 200 cm (24 to 79 in); silt loam; greenish black (10Y 2.5/1) broken face moist, dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1) broken face dry; massive; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; moderately fluid; very few very fine roots at top of horizon; common very fine irregular pores; 10.3 mS of EC; slight sulfurous odor; reduced iron monosulfides present; positive to alpha-alpha dipyridyl measured in matrix; noneffervescent; slightly acid (pH 6.3), very strongly acid after 16 weeks (oxidized pH 4.6)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These are the Akulik River and Ryanlake soils.
Akulik River soils lack ponding, lack sulfidic materials, have a cambic horizon, have a coarse-loamy particle size class, have a gelic soil temperature regime, have occasional, brief flooding, and occur on talfs of delta plains
Ryanlake soils lack ponding, have permafrost, have cryoturbation, have a thick organic surface, have a gelic soil temperature regime, have an isotic mineralogy class, have a clay range of 2 to 10 percent, have occasional, brief flooding, and occur on treads of terraces
