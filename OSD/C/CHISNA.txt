CHISNA SERIES

Depth class: very deep
Drainage class: well drained
Parent material: silty loess over sandy and gravelly till
Landscape: foothills
Landform: hillslopes
Slopes: 0 to 20 percent
Mean annual precipitation: about 600 mm (24 in)
Mean annual air temperature: about -3 degrees C. (27 degrees F)
Frost-free period: 32 to 71 days

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-loamy, mixed, superactive, nonacid, subgelic Turbic Humigelepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Chisna highly organic silt loam in ericaceous and willow scrub on a linear, linear summit on a slope of 2 percent at an elevation of 1221m (4005 ft).

	A--0 to 7 cm (0.0 to 3 in); silt loam, black (5Y 2.5/2) moist; moderate coarse subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; common very fine and fine roots throughout; very fine irregular pores; 10 percent subrounded cobbles by volume; strongly acid (pH 5.4); clear wavy boundary (6 to 11 cm, 3 to 4 in).

	Ajj--7 to 20 cm (3 to 8 in); silt loam, dark olive brown (2.5Y 3/3) moist; moderate coarse subangular blocky parts to weak fine platy structure; slightly hard, friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; few very fine and fine roots throughout; very fine vesicular pores; 5 percent subrounded cobbles by volume; moderately acid (pH 5.6); clear wavy boundary (12 to 17 cm, 5 to 7 in).

	Ejj--20 to 28 cm (8 to 11 in); silt loam, olive brown (2.5Y 4/4) moist; weak coarse platy parts to weak fine granular structure; soft, very friable, slightly sticky, slightly plastic; nonsmeary; few very fine and fine roots throughout; fine irregular pores; 5 percent subrounded cobbles by volume; moderately acid (pH 5.8); clear wavy boundary (7 to 12 cm, 3 to 5 in).

	2C1--28 to 49 cm (11 to 19 in); cobbly sandy loam, olive brown (2.5Y 4/3) moist; weak medium granular structure; soft, very friable, slightly sticky, slightly plastic; nonsmeary; few very fine roots throughout; fine irregular pores; 25 percent subrounded cobbles by volume; moderately acid (pH 6.0); clear wavy boundary (16 to 22 cm, 6 to 9 in).

	2C2--49 to 150 cm (19 to 59 in); gravelly sandy loam, olive gray (5Y 4/2) moist; soft, very friable, slightly sticky, slightly plastic; nonsmeary; fine irregular pores; 15 percent subrounded gravel by volume; slightly acid (pH 6.2); clear wavy boundary (88 to 109 cm, 35 to 43 in).

TYPE LOCATION: North Copper River Soil Survey Area, Alaska; latitude 63.08731, longitude -145.37425, UTM 6865681.19 north, and UTM 481346.19 east, zone 6, Datum WGS84.

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These are the Ahtell and Chelina series
Ahtell soils are well drained, lack permafrost, have folistic and umbric epipedons, and occur on hill tops, and hillside positions. 
Chelina soils are well drained, lack an umbric epipedon, and occur on lacustrine terraces.

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:

Runoff: low
