VANDEVENTER SERIES

Depth class: Very deep
Drainage class: Well drained
Saturated hydraulic conductivity: Moderately high to high 
Parent material: Human-transported material mostly derived from nearby loess deposits and overlies Peoria loess, where it is present.
Geomorphic location: Leveled land and fills on hillslopes and interfluves of hills
Slope range: 0 to 10
Mean annual temperature: 12 degrees C
Mean annual precipitation: 1050 millimeters 

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, artifactic, mixed, superactive, nonacid, mesic Anthroportic Udorthents

TYPICAL PEDON: Vandeventer silt loam, 2 percent slope on a former residential lot where human artifacts from the demolition of buildings are buried and the site was leveled. Site currently utilized as a community garden. (Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise noted.)

 ^Au--0 to 22 centimeters; 80 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/6) and 20 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/6) silt loam; weak fine subangular blocky parting to moderate thin platy structure; friable; many very fine roots and common fine roots throughout; common very fine interstitial, many fine interstitial, and common fine dendritic tubular pores; 3 percent fine prominent gray (10YR 6/1), moist, relict iron depletions; 2 percent gravel-sized brick fragments; neutral (6.6 pH); abrupt smooth boundary (6 to 24 centimeters thick)

 ^Cu1--22 to 34 centimeters; 60 percent light gray (7.5YR 7/1) and 20 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) and 20 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/6) extremely artifactual loam; massive; firm; common very fine roots throughout; common very fine dendritic tubular pores; 3 percent gravel-sized bitumen (asphalt) fragments, 40 percent gravel-sized brick fragments, and 20 percent gravel-sized concrete fragments; slightly alkaline (7.7 pH); abrupt smooth boundary 

 ^Cu2--34 to 45 centimeters; dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) very artifactual silty clay loam; relict weak medium subangular blocky structure; firm; few fine roots throughout; common very fine interstitial pores; 20 percent fine distinct yellowish brown (10YR 5/6), moist, iron-manganese masses throughout; 5 percent gravel-sized bitumen (asphalt) fragments, 10 percent gravel-sized concrete fragments, and 30 percent gravel-sized brick fragments; slightly alkaline (7.7 pH); clear smooth boundary.

 ^Cu3--45 to 65 centimeters; 90 percent brown (10YR 4/3) and 10 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/6) extremely artifactual loamy sand; massive; firm; common very fine roots throughout; few very fine interstitial pores; 25 percent gravel-sized concrete fragments, 10 percent gravel-sized bitumen (asphalt) fragments, and 35 percent by volume gravel-sized brick fragments; moderately alkaline (8.1 pH); clear smooth boundary

 ^Cu4--65 to 93 centimeters; 50 percent dark brown (10YR 3/3) and 50 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) very artifactual sandy loam; massive; firm; few very fine roots throughout and very few (0.1) medium roots throughout; few very fine interstitial pores; 2 percent channer-sized glass fragments, 3 gravel sized untreated wood fragments, 5 percent gravel-sized bitumen (asphalt) fragments, 10 percent gravel-sized concrete fragments, and 35 percent gravel-sized brick; moderately alkaline (8.0 pH); clear smooth boundary

 ^Cu5--93 to 118 centimeters; 80 percent dark brown (10YR 3/3) and 20 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/6) very artifactual silty clay loam; massive; firm; few very fine roots throughout; few very fine vesicular pores; 5 percent cobble-sized concrete fragments, 12 percent gravel-sized  brick fragments, and 20 percent gravel-sized bitumen (asphalt)fragments; slightly alkaline (7.6 pH); gradual wavy boundary (Combined thickness of ^C horizons is greater than 95 centimeters)

 2C1--118 to 154 centimeters; 70 percent dark brown (10YR 3/3) and 20 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) and 10 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/6) silty clay loam; massive; firm; prominent light gray (10YR 7/2), dry, silt coats; 2 percent very fine distinct black (10YR 2/1) manganese masses and 3 percent fine distinct gray (10YR 6/1) iron depletions; neutral (7.1 pH); clear smooth boundary

 2C2--154 to 200 centimeters (60.6 to 78.7 inches); 80 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 3/4) and 20 percent dark yellowish brown (10YR 4/6) silty clay loam; massive; firm; 2 percent very fine distinct black (10YR 2/1) manganese masses; neutral (6.7 pH)

COMPETING SERIES: These are the Sauken soils. 
Sauken soils have aquic conditions within 100cm of the soil surface, typically contain glauconite pellets, and are typically formed in filled marshlands and drainageways.

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Parent material: Human-transported material over loess
Landscape: Hills
Landform: Hillslopes and interfluves  
Anthropogenic landform: Leveled land where demolition of human structures and subsequent grading of soil and burying human artifacts has occurred.
Slope: 0 to 12 percent
Elevation: 120 to 250 meters
Frost-free period: 184 to 228 days
Mean annual temperature: 11 to 14 degrees C
Mean annual precipitation: 940 to 1194 millimeters

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These are the Harvester, Shaw, and Winfield soils. 

Harvester soils have less than 15 percent by volume human artifacts in the particle size control section; found on similar anthropogenic landforms.
Shaw soils have aquic conditions as high as 40 centimeters from the surface and have fewer human artifacts in the control section; found on similar anthropogenic landforms.
Winfield soils do not have human-transported materials and are formed in loess that is both native and intact; found on hillslopes and interfluves.
