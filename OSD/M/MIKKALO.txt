MIKKALO SERIES

Landscape-- hills, plateaus, canyonlands
Landform--hillslopes, structural benches, canyons
Slope--0 to 60 percent
Parent material--loess and a small amount of volcanic ash in the upper part
Mean annual precipitation--about 250 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 10 degrees C
Depth class--moderately deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--aridic
Soil temperature regime--mesic
Soil moisture subclass--xeric

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-silty, mixed, superactive, mesic Calcidic Haploxerolls

TYPICAL PEDON: Mikkalo silt loam in an area of grassland

 A1--0 to 8 cm; silt loam, grayish brown (10YR 5/2) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; many very fine roots; many very fine tubular pores; neutral (pH 6.8); gradual wavy boundary

 A2--8 to 28 cm; silt loam, grayish brown (10YR 5/2) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; many very fine roots; many very fine tubular pores; neutral (pH 6.8); gradual wavy boundary

 Bw1--28 to 41 cm; silt loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; many very fine roots; many very fine tubular pores; slightly alkaline (pH 7.4); gradual wavy boundary

 Bw2--41 to 69 cm; silt loam, pale brown (10YR 6/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; many very fine roots; many very fine tubular pores; slightly alkaline (pH 7.6); gradual smooth boundary

 Bk1--69 to 89 cm; silt loam, very pale brown (10YR 7/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; weak medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; common very fine roots; many very fine tubular pores; 5 percent gravel 2 to 10 mm in diameter; slightly effervescent; slightly alkaline (pH 7.8); abrupt wavy boundary

 Bk2--89 to 97 cm; silt loam, very pale brown (10YR 8/2) dry, brown (10YR 5/3) moist; massive; slightly hard, friable, slightly sticky and slightly plastic; few very fine roots; few very fine tubular pores; 15 percent gravel 2 to 10 mm in diameter; strongly effervescent; few streaks and splotches of soft powdery lime; moderately alkaline (pH 8.4); gradual wavy boundary

 2R--97 cm; lime-coated basalt

TYPE LOCATION: Gilliam County, Oregon; about 6 m north of gravel road in the SE1/4SE1/4NE1/4 of section 19, T. 1 S., R. 21 E.

RANGE IN CHARACTERISTICS:

Thickness of mollic epipedon--18 to 41 cm
Particle-size control section--silt loam; 8 to 12 percent clay; less than 15 percent material coarser than very fine sand
Depth to secondary carbonates--41 to 75 cm
