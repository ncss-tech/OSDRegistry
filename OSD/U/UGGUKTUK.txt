UGGUKTUK SERIES

Depth class: moderately deep to lithic bedrock
Drainage class: somewhat poorly drained
Parent material: gravelly loamy residuum weathered from graywacke
Landscape: mountains, hills
Landform: saddles, broad ridges
Slope: 1 to 15 percent
Mean annual precipitation: about 500 mm (20 in)
Mean annual air temperature: about -4 degrees C. (25 degrees F.)
Frost-free period: 70 to 90 days

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, mixed, superactive, acid Aeric Cryaquepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Ugguktuk gravelly silt loam in a wet alpine tundra community on a linear, linear summit on a slope of 4 percent at an elevation of 516m (1695 ft)

     A--0 to 13 cm (0 to 5 in); gravelly silt loam; very dark brown (10YR 2/2) broken face moist, dark grayish brown (10YR 4/2) broken face dry; weak medium subangular blocky parts to weak very fine granular structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; common very fine and fine roots throughout; few very fine dendritic tubular pores; 13 percent gravel and 3 percent cobbles; extremely acid (pH 4.3); abrupt wavy boundary. (6 to 18 cm, 2 to 7 in thick)

     Bw--13 to 37 cm (5 to 15 in); very gravelly silt loam; dark brown (10YR 3/3) broken face moist, pale brown (10YR 6/3) broken face dry; weak medium subangular blocky parts to weak fine granular structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; moderately few very fine roots throughout; few very fine dendritic tubular pores; 40 percent gravel; very strongly acid (pH 4.9); clear wavy boundary. (7 to 30 cm, 3 to 12 in thick)

     Bg--37 to 61 cm (15 to 24 in); extremely gravelly silt loam; dark gray (10YR 4/1) broken face moist, gray (10YR 6/1) broken face dry; weak medium subangular blocky structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; few very few very fine roots throughout; few very fine dendritic tubular pores; 65 percent gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.1); gradual wavy boundary. (12 to 30 cm, 5 to 12 in thick)

     Cg--61 to 90 cm (24 to 35 in); extremely gravelly silt loam; dark gray (10YR 4/1) moist, gray (10YR 6/1) dry; massive; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky, nonplastic; nonsmeary; 80 percent gravel; strongly acid (pH 5.2). (25 to 45 cm, 10 to 18 in thick)

     R--90 to 115 cm (35 to 45 in); indurated graywacke bedrock.

TYPE LOCATION: Nulato Hills - Continental Divide and Blackburn Hills Soil Survey Area, Alaska; latitude 64.4914166, longitude -160.2726944, datum WGS84, UTM north 7152388.97 and UTM east 438848.51, zone 4, datum WGS84.

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS: These are the Anugi, Apkun, Atkaktuk, Continentaldivide, Debauch, Iksrukkaa, and Tatkik soils.
Anugi soils have a lithic contact at depths between 150 to 200 cm (60 to 80 in), lack aquic conditions, lack a reduced matrix, have a pH range of 3.5 to 6.5, and occur on backslopes and mountainflanks, upper third
Apkun soils lack aquic conditions, lack a reduced matrix, have an umbric epipedon, lack a cambic horizon, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on mountainflanks, upper and center thirds
Atkaktuk soils lack a lithic contact within 200 cm (80 in), have a fragmental layer starting at depths between 35 to 60 cm (14 to 24 in), lack aquic conditions, lack a reduced matrix, have an umbric epipedon, lack a cambic horizon, have an loamy-skeletal over fragmental particle size class, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on mountainflanks, upper thirds
Continentaldivide soils have a lithic contact at depths between 20 to 50 cm (8 to 20 in), lack aquic conditions, lack a reduced matrix, have a mollic epipedon, lack a cambic horizon, have a nonacid reaction class, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on ridges
Debauch soils have a paralithic contact at depths between 20 to 50 cm (8 to 20 in), lack aquic conditions, lack a reduced matrix, have an ochric epipedon, have a cambic horizon, have a nonacid reaction class, have a gelic soil temperature regime, and occur on ridges
Iksrukkaa soils have a paralithic contact at depths between 50 to 100 cm (20 to 40 in), lack aquic conditions, lack a reduced matrix, have a folistic epipedon, lack a cambic horizon, and occur on side slopes, backslopes, and shoulders
Tatkik soils have redox concentrations starting within 45 cm (18 in), have a nonacid reaction class, and occur on swales on mountainflanks

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY: 
Drainage class: somewhat poorly drained
Saturation during normal years: saturated between 25 and 50 cm (10 and 20 in) for less than one month during the growing season
Saturated Hydraulic Conductivity: moderately high in the A, Bw, Bg, and Cg horizons
Permeability: moderate in the A, Bw, Bg, and Cg horizons
Runoff: very high
