BAREFLAT SERIES

Landscape--Intermontane Basin
Landform--hills, dipslopes, scarpslopes
Slope--2 to 35 percent
Parent material--sandy volcanic ash and colluvium and residuum derived and weathered from andesite
Mean annual precipitation--about 710 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 6.4 degrees C
Depth class--moderately deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--xeric
Soil temperature regime--frigid
Soil moisture subclass--typic

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Ashy, glassy, frigid Vitrandic Haploxerepts

TYPICAL PEDON: Bareflat ashy loamy sand on a forested dipslope with 0.2 percent surface cover of stones and boulders, a slope of 10 percent, and an elevation of 1827 m

 Oe--0 to 3 cm; moderately decomposed plant material; abrupt smooth boundary

 A--3 to 17 cm; ashy loamy sand, reddish brown (5YR 4/4) dry, very dark brown (7.5YR 2.5/2) moist; weak fine granular structure; soft, very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine and fine, and few medium roots; common fine irregular and dendritic tubular pores; 2 percent andesite gravel; noneffervescent; slightly acid (pH 6.4); clear smooth boundary

 Bw1--17 to 52 centimeters; ashy sandy loam, yellowish red (5YR 4/6) dry, dark reddish brown (5YR 3/3) moist; moderate medium subangular blocky structure; slightly hard, very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; common very fine and fine, few medium and coarse, and common very coarse roots; few fine and medium dendritic tubular pores; 5 percent andesite gravel; noneffervescent; neutral (pH 6.6); clear wavy boundary

 Bw2--52 to 75 centimeters; ashy sandy loam, reddish brown (5YR 4/4) dry, dark reddish brown (5YR 3/3) moist; weak coarse subangular blocky parting to moderate medium subangular blocky structure; moderately hard, very friable, nonsticky, nonplastic; few fine and medium roots; few fine dendritic tubular pores; 10 percent andesite gravel and 2 percent andesite cobbles; noneffervescent; moderately acid (pH 6.0); abrupt wavy boundary

 R--75 cm; indurated, fractured andesite; fractures 12 to 20 cm apart; few fine and medium roots in cracks; noneffervescent.

TYPE LOCATION: Lake County, Oregon; about 305 m south and 454 m east of the northwest corner of sec. 6, T.37S., R.17E., Willamette Meridian; Gearhart Mountain, Oregon USGS quadrangle; Latitude 42.393460 degrees north, longitude -120.757600 degrees west, datum WGS84 (coordinates derived from GPS unit)
