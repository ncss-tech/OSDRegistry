BEDGROUND SERIES

Landscape--plateaus
Landform--structural benches
Slope--2 to 35 percent
Parent material--loess over basalt
Mean annual precipitation--about 230 mm
Mean annual air temperature--about 10 degrees C
Depth class--deep
Drainage class--well drained
Soil moisture regime--aridic
Soil temperature regime--mesic
Soil moisture subclass--xeric

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-silty, mixed, superactive, mesic Xeric Haplocambids

TYPICAL PEDON: Bedground silt loam in an area of rangeland, on a 4-percent, north-facing, linear, convex slope at an elevation of 490 m (All textures are apparent field textures.)

 A--0 to 10 cm; silt loam, pale brown (10YR 6/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; moderate thick and medium platy structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky and nonplastic; common fine, medium, and coarse roots; neutral (pH 7.0); abrupt smooth boundary

 Bw1--10 to 38 cm; silt loam, pale brown (10YR 6/3) dry, dark brown (10YR 3/3) moist; weak medium prismatic structure parting to moderate medium and fine subangular blocky; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky and nonplastic; common fine, medium, and coarse roots; slightly alkaline (pH 7.7); gradual smooth boundary

 Bw2--38 to 66 cm; silt loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; weak medium prismatic structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky and nonplastic; common fine, medium, and coarse roots; slightly alkaline (pH 7.7); abrupt wavy boundary

 Bk1--66 to 86 cm; silt loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; weak medium prismatic structure; friable, slightly hard, nonsticky and nonplastic; common fine, medium, and coarse roots; slightly effervescent, disseminated carbonates; slightly alkaline (pH 7.7); abrupt broken boundary

 Bk2--86 to 112 cm; silt loam, brown (10YR 5/3) dry, brown (10YR 4/3) moist; massive; very friable, soft, nonsticky and nonplastic; common fine, medium, and coarse roots; violently effervescent, disseminated carbonates; strongly alkaline (pH 8.5); abrupt wavy boundary

 R--112 to 127 cm; indurated basalt

TYPE LOCATION: Yakama Nation, Yakima County, Washington, about 1 km northeast of Sampson Cemetery; about 300 m south and 370 m west of the northeast corner of section 31, T. 8 N., R. 21 E.; latitude 46.1403828, longitude -120.2279833, datum WGS 84 (coordinates estimated from section, township, and range)

RANGE IN CHARACTERISTICS:
Mean annual soil temperature--10 to 13 degrees C
Soil moisture--continuously dry in all parts between depths of 10 and 30 cm from about May 1 to October 1
Particle-size control section--averages 5 to 10 percent clay and less than 15 percent sand coarser than very fine sand
Organic matter content in upper 20 cm (after mixing)--less than 1 percent
Depth to bedrock--100 to 150 cm
Depth to secondary carbonates (where present)--50 to 100 cm

A horizon
Value--5 or 6 dry, 2 or 3 moist
Chroma--2 or 3 moist or dry
Thickness--5 to 18 cm

Bw horizon
Value--5 or 6 dry, 3 or 4 moist
Chroma--2 to 4 moist or dry
Reaction--7.4 to 8.4
Combined thickness--33 to 89 cm

Bk horizon
Hue--10YR, 2.5Y
Value--5 to 8 dry, 4 to 6 moist
Chroma--3 to 4 moist or dry
Texture--silt loam, very fine sandy loam
Calcium carbonate equivalent--2 to 15 percent
Reaction--7.4 to 9.0
Combined thickness--38 to 76 cm

COMPETING SERIES:
Sagemoor, Shano, Warden--carbonates within a depth of 38 cm of the soil surface

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Elevation--300 to 610 m
Climate--semiarid; warm, dry summers; cool, moist winters
Mean annual precipitation--150 to 250 mm
Mean annual air temperature--8 to 12 degrees C
Frost-free period--120 to 200 days

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
Kiona--on uplands; loamy-skeletal particle-size class; no lithic contact within a depth of more than 150 cm (very deep)
Prosser--on plateaus; coarse-loamy particle-size class; 50 to 100 cm (moderately deep) to a lithic contact (basalt)
Rock Creek--on uplands; clayey-skeletal particle-size class; 20 to 50 cm (very shallow, shallow) to a lithic contact (basalt)
Shano--on uplands; no lithic contact within a depth of more than 150 cm (very deep)

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--well drained
Saturated hydraulic conductivity (Ksat)--moderately high

USE AND VEGETATION:
Use--livestock grazing
Native vegetation--bluebunch wheatgrass, beardless wheatgrass, Sandberg bluegrass, Wyoming big sagebrush

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: Central Washington; MLRA 8; small extent

SOIL SURVEY REGIONAL OFFICE (SSRO) RESPONSIBLE: Portland, Oregon

SERIES ESTABLISHED: Yakama Nation, Yakima County, Washington; 2014
