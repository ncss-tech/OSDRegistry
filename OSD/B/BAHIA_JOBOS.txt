BAHIA JOBOS SERIES

MLRA(s)--273
Depth Class--Very deep
Drainage Class--Subaqueous drainage
Water depth range--0 to 400 centimeters
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately low to very high
Parent Material--Coarse-loamy lagoonal deposits 
Geomorphic location--Flood-tidal deltas 
Slope--0 to 1 percent
Mean Annual Air Temperature: 26 degrees C (79 degrees F)
Mean Annual Water Temperature--28 degrees C (82 degrees F)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Coarse-loamy, mixed, subactive, nonacid, isohyperthermic Typic Fluviwassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Bahia Jobos sandy clay loam on a flood-tidal delta slope in the inland side protected from the sea behind barrier islands of a coastal lagoon. (Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated).

 Aseg--0 to 12 centimeters; dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1), sandy clay loam; structureless, massive; nonfluid; strong sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, strong reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderately alkaline (pH 8.4), slightly alkaline (pH 7.6) oxidized pH; clear boundary.

 ACseg--12 to 28 centimeters; dark gray (5Y 4/1), shelly sandy clay loam; 30 percent Halimeda calcifications; structureless, massive; nonfluid; moderate sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, moderate reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), slightly alkaline (pH 7.7) oxidized pH; clear boundary.

 Cseg1--28 to 108 centimeters; gray (5Y 5/1), coarse sandy loam; 5 percent shell fragment and 5 percent Halimeda calcifications; structureless, massive; nonfluid; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, slight reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide;  moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), slightly alkaline (pH 7.8) oxidized pH; gradual boundary.

 Cseg2--108 to 226 centimeters; greenish gray (10Y 5/1), shelly coarse sandy loam; 5 percent shell fragments and 20 percent Halimeda calcifications; structureless, massive; nonfluid; slight sulfurous odor; no reaction to 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, no reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), slightly alkaline (pH 7.8); clear boundary.

 Cseg3--226 to 246 centimeters; greenish gray (10Y 5/1), coarse sandy loam; 2 percent shell fragments and 5 percent Halimeda calcifications; structureless, massive; nonfluid; slight sulfurous odor; no reaction to 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, no reaction to 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), slightly alkaline (pH 7.8).

TYPE LOCATION: Salinas municipality, Puerto Rico; Bahia Jobos Lagoon, approximately 4.5 kilometers (2.8 miles) south-southwest from the Jobos Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve (JBNERR) central building and approximately 2.4 kilometers (1.5 miles) west-southwest of Cayo Caribe southernmost shore point.

GEOGRAPHIC SETTING:
Parent Material--Coarse-loamy lagoonal deposits 
Landscape-- Coastal lagoons in semiarid coastal plain, Puerto Rico
Landform--Flood-tidal delta slopes (primary), flats, and channels
Mean Annual Air Temperature--22 to 31 degrees C (72 to 88 degrees F)
Mean Annual Precipitation--508 to 1,145 millimeters (20 to 45 inches)
Mean Annual Water Temperature--26 to 32 degrees C (79 to 90 degrees F)
Water salinity range--28 to 43 parts per thousand

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Aguas Llanas, Caricoche and Majaguas soils. 
Aguas Llanas soils--are very similar to Bahia Jobos but have finer textures, are slightly higher on the landscape and occur on flood-tidal delta flats.
Caricoche soils--have coarser textures with reef fragments. They occur on the flood-tidal delta channels where there are greater tidal currents leading to increased reef fragment accumulation.
Majaguas soils--are on slightly lower landscape positions and have finer textures throughout

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous (permanently submersed)
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately low to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic 
Soil is continuously inundated by saline water

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major Uses--Used for wildlife habitat, recreational boating, fishing, and swimming, marinas, mooring fields, and commercial shellfish habitat.  These soils provide improved water quality and flood / storm surge water storage.
Dominant Vegetation--Turtle grass (Thalasia testudinum), Syringodium filiforme (manatee grass), and watercress alga (Halimeda opuntia).

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: 
General area--Coastal lagoons, coasts, and bays of Puerto Rico.
Land Resource Region--Z - Caribbean Region
MLRA--273 - Semiarid Coastal Plains

SOIL SURVEY REGIONAL OFFICE (SSRO) RESPONSIBLE: Special Projects Office

SERIES ESTABLISHED: Salinas Municipality, Puerto Rico; 2025. The name is from the Bahia Jobos Lagoon, one of the biggest most important bays in Puerto Rico, where it was originally recognized.

REMARKS: This subaqueous series is mapped in areas previously published as water in Puerto Rico Soil Survey publications.

Diagnostic horizons and features recognized in this pedon:
Ocrhic epipedon--the zone 0 to 28 centimeters (0 to 11 inches).
Oxidized pH values--the shell fragments and Halimeda materials that occur throughout this soil buffer the oxidized pH preventing hypersulfidic soil material development
Peraquic soil moisture regime--Positive soil water potential (permanently submerged) at the soil surface. (under 0 to 4 meters of salt water).
Haplic feature--soil in nonfluid at least in some layer from 20 to 50 centimeters.
