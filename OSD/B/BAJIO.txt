BAJIO SERIES

MLRA(s)--273
Depth Class--Shallow
Drainage Class--Subaqueous drainage
Water depth range--0 to 100 centimeters
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately low to very high
Parent Material--Loamy-skeletal marine deposits over coral limestone bedrock
Geomorphic location--Reef 
Slope--0 to 1 percent
Mean Annual Air Temperature--26 degrees C (79 degrees F)
Mean Annual Water Temperature--28 degrees C (82 degrees F)

TAXONOMIC CLASS: Loamy-skeletal, mixed, subactive, nonacid, isohyperthermic Lithic Fluviwassents

TYPICAL PEDON: Bajio shelly sandy loam in a reef platform facing the Caribbean sea. (Colors are for moist soil unless otherwise stated).

 ACseg--0 to 5 centimeters; greenish black (10Y 2.5/1), shelly sandy loam; structureless, massive; nonfluid, nonsticky, nonplastic; no sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, strong effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderately alkaline (pH 8.3), moderately alkaline (pH 7.9) oxidized pH; clear boundary.

 Cseg1--5 to 35 centimeters; very dark greenish gray (10Y 3/1), very gravelly sandy loam; 50 percent coral fragments; structureless, massive; nonfluid, nonsticky, nonplastic; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, strong effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide;  moderately alkaline (pH 8.4), moderately alkaline (pH 8.3) oxidized pH; clear boundary.

 Cseg2--35 to 49 centimeters; dark greenish gray (10Y 4/1), very gravelly sandy loam; 50 percent coral fragments, 5 percent shell fragments; structureless, massive; nonfluid, nonsticky, nonplastic; slight sulfurous odor; color change with 3 percent hydrogen peroxide, moderate effervescence with 30 percent hydrogen peroxide; moderately alkaline (pH 8.4), moderately alkaline (pH 8.3) oxidized pH; very abrupt boundary.

 R--49 centimeters; limestone bedrock (coral reef), indurated bedrock.

TYPE LOCATION: Salinas municipality, Puerto Rico. East-southeast of the eastern most portion of Cayo Palmas on a reef platform, approximately 1.8 kilometers (1.1 miles) south of Camino del Indio trail (trail through the Las Mareas community) off the coast and approximately 0.6 kilometers (0.4 miles) west of Cayo Isla Perdida covered by subaquatic vegetation.

Landform--Reef
Mean Annual Air Temperature: 22 to 31 degrees C (71 to 88 degrees F)

GEOGRAPHICALLY ASSOCIATED SOILS:
These are the Caricoche soils:
Caricoche soils--Occur on the flood-tidal delta channels which drain the reef platform areas of the wave currents. They are also skeletal but are very deep and more fluid throughout. 

DRAINAGE AND SATURATED HYDRAULIC CONDUCTIVITY:
Drainage class--Subaqueous (permanently submersed)
Saturated hydraulic conductivity--Moderately low to very high
Soil moisture regime--Peraquic 
Soil is continuously inundated by saline water

USE AND VEGETATION:
Major Uses--Used for wildlife habitat, fishing, and commercial shellfish habitat.  These soils provide improved water quality and flood / storm surge water storage.
Dominant Vegetation--Common sub-aquatic vegetation (SAV) species include Thalassia (Turtle grass), Syringodium (manatee grass), halimeda (watercress algae), and Halofila (paddle grass). 

DISTRIBUTION AND EXTENT: 
General area--Located along coasts and bays of Puerto Rico.
Land Resource Region--Z - Caribbean Region
MLRA--273 - Semiarid Coastal Plains
Extent--Small

SOIL SURVEY REGIONAL OFFICE (SSRO) RESPONSIBLE: SPECIAL PROJECTS OFFICE

SERIES ESTABLISHED: Salinas Municipality, Puerto Rico; 2025. The name Bajio (a shallow) refers to the shallow water landform (reef) which can sometimes almost be exposed.

REMARKS: This subaqueous series is mapped in areas previously published as water in Puerto Rico Soil Survey publications.

Diagnostic horizons and features recognized in this pedon--
Peraquic soil moisture regime--Positive soil water potential (permanently submerged) at the soil surface. 
Ochric epipedon - the zone 0 to 5 centimeters (0 to 2 inches).
Haplic feature - soil in nonfluid at least in some layer from 20 to 50 centimeters.
Loamy-Skeletal - Zone from 0 to 49 centimeters have an average of 35 percent or more coral fragments.
Lithic contact - limestone coral bedrock at less than 50 centimeters from surface
Oxidized pH values--the shell fragments and Halimeda materials that occur throughout this soil buffer the oxidized pH preventing potential hypersulfidic materials from developing
